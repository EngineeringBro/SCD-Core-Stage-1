To maintain accurate reporting and consistent data, RepairQ requires that all devices and items attached to tickets are created as Catalog items first. This ensures that your reports are based on consistent data. To avoid duplicate entries, inconsistencies, or errors, it’s important to establish a system for managing your sale, repair, and trade-in devices before adding them to your catalog. A well-organized catalog simplifies ticket creation and ensures seamless tracking for both sales and repairs.

Sale Tickets (Ticket Items)

Any item in your catalog can be added to a ticket as a sale item. Here’s how items are typically managed:

  • Services - These are generally non-inventoried since they don’t involve physical stock.

  • Physical Items - Items that hold physical stock in your store are marked as Inventoried. Inventoried Items must have an active stock level to add and close a ticket. When an inventoried item has zero stock, it will be added to the ticket as a Backorder Item. The ticket cannot be closed until the backordered item is fulfilled with active inventory. This ensures accurate stock tracking and prevents overselling.

    • If you choose not to use RepairQ for inventory management, you can create non-inventoried items without tracking stock levels.

  • Serialized Items - Serialized items are inventoried products that have unique serial numbers, such as smartphones, tablets, or accessories with identifiable codes. To track these serial numbers in RepairQ, the item must be marked as Serialized in the catalog.

Repair Tickets (Repair Devices)

To add an item as the Repair Device on a repair ticket, catalog item must be marked as Repairable. When creating these items, you can tailor them to fit your record-keeping preferences. Whether you prefer detailed or general entries, both approaches can work effectively as long as you're consistent.

  • Specific Device Details - If you prefer detailed records for Quick Sale and Repair tickets, create catalog items with specific attributes (e.g., iPhone 13 Pro 128GB Green). Mark these items as both Inventoried and Repairable. When a customer comes in, simply select the specific catalog device to add to the repair.

  • General Device Details for Repairs - For a more streamlined approach to repairs, you can create generic catalog items (e.g., iPhone 13 Pro). Mark these items as Repairable but not Inventoried. During the intake process, you can add the generic catalog item to the repair ticket and include the specific device details in the description section.

Trade-in Devices (Trade-in Tickets)

Trade-in devices are Inventoried items that your business accepts from customers in exchange for payment. These items are added to your inventory upon receipt, allowing you to track them as any other inventoried product.

  • Trade-in Restrictions - Users can restrict Trade-in Items to items that are both Inventoried and Repairable, to ensure that only eligible devices are traded in.

    • Navigate to Company Settings → Catalog → General Settings to locate the setting: Only allow trade-in items for catalog items that are repairable (and inventoried).

Assign Catalog Item Behaviors

This is a condensed example creating new catalog items, focused on Catalog Item Behaviors, and how they relate to various ticket types. For detailed instructions, see Creating a Catalog Item.

Note: For more information on importing catalog items, see Importing Catalog Items Into RepairQ.

On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Catalog. On the Catalog List screen, click the Add button on the top-right of the page.

Begin filling out the details for the new item. When creating a catalog item, you have options that will determine whether the item can be added to Quick Sale, Repair, and Trade-in tickets. These options are located under the Behaviors section of the page.

Note: Certain attributes lock after associating with an inventory record or ticket to maintain data integrity and retain consistency for reporting purposes.

  • Repairable - If this is selected, this item can be attached as the Repair Device on a Repair ticket. This setting can be activated/deactivated at any time.

    • With so many models of devices out there, it may be difficult to keep track of aging and rare modes in your catalog. You can create a catch-all item for these exceptions. Creating a generic or "other" catalog item will allow you to add an item to a Repair ticket and still be able to add a description for the "other" details in the Description field of the Repair Item.

  • Inventoried - If this is selected, this item will hold an Instock quantity in the system. This means you will need to replenish quantities as needed in order to sell these items on tickets. Inventoried items can also be added as an intake device on Trade-in tickets. The trade-in process enters the device into your inventory when it is purchased from the customer.

    • This attribute locks (whether selected or deselected) after the item has been associated with a ticket, PO, or inventory record.

  • Serialized - If this is selected, this will require you to enter a serial number for the device when adding into inventory, Trade-in tickets, receiving Purchase Orders, inventory counts, and inventory imports.

    • This attribute locks (whether selected or deselected) after the item has been associated with a ticket, PO, or inventory record.

Importing Catalog Items

You can also use the Catalog Item Import feature to create new devices in bulk. When importing Catalog Items with the provided template, you can update the field under the Repairable column to "1" to mark it as repairable. This also works for Inventoried and Serialized fields.
