A recent RepairQ update reworks its browser cookie logic. Because of this, some browsers may experience a loop when attempting to login. To fix this, the old cookie needs to be deleted so a new one can generate.

🍪 How to Delete a Specific Cookie from a Website

Use the steps below to delete a cookie just for one site (not all cookies).

🔵 Google Chrome

  1. Open Chrome and go to the website.

  2. Click the icon(lock or two lines with circles) to the left of the URL bar.

  3. Click "Cookies and site data".

  4. In the popup, click "Manage on-site device data”.

  5. You’ll see a list of cookies grouped by domain.

  6. Find repairq.io

  7. Click "Remove" or the trash can icon.

  8. Refresh the page to apply the change.

🟣 Safari (macOS)

Safari doesn't have a lock icon with cookie access like Chrome or Edge. You’ll need to go through Preferences.

  1. Open Safari and go to the website

  2. Open Safari and go to Safari → Settings (or press Cmd + ,).

  3. Click the "Privacy" tab.

  4. Click "Manage Website Data..."

  5. In the search bar, type repairq.io.

  6. Select repairq.io from the list.

  7. Click "Remove"

  8. Click "Done".

  9. Refresh the page.

🔵 Microsoft Edge

  1. Open Edge and navigate to the website.

  2. Click the lock icon to the left of the URL bar.

  3. Click "Cookies and Site Data" then select “Cookies”.

  4. In the popup, you’ll see a list of websites.

  5. Select repairq.io

  6. Click "Remove" or the trash icon.

  7. Click "Done", then refresh the site.
