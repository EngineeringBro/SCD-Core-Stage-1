Occasionally, an item will be defective during initial inspection upon receiving the item into your inventory. A Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA) is a part of the Reverse Logistics cycle, where defective products are returned to the manufacturer or vendor during the warranty period. When items have been sent out, they will be marked as RMA Sent in your inventory. Once the Supplier/Vendor has contacted you about compensation/replacement of these items, the final step is to reconcile the RMA items in your inventory.

Reconcile an Individual RMA Item

1. On the Navigation Bar, go to Inventory → RMA Tracking.

2. On the Tracking page, change the filter status to RMA Sent and Apply. This will list all of the returns that are awaiting reconciliation. You can also use the filters to search for a specific Supplier you received a response from.

3. Once you have filtered the list for your results, click the checkbox to select the item(s) you are ready to reconcile. Once you have made your selection(s), click the Edit Selected button. From the list of options, click Reconcile Selected.

4. A "Reconciled Selected" pop-up will appear. You will need to choose how this item should be reconciled. In the Movement field, select the appropriate status to move the item(s) based on the resolution the supplier has provided. Add any additional notes for the inventory items if necessary, and click Reconcile to submit and save your changes.

  • RMA Accepted (RMA Credit) - Use this status if the Supplier has provided you with compensation (either a refund or credit) for the defective item.

  • RMA Rejected (Damaged) - Use this status if the Supplier has rejected the return. This will place the item into Damaged status and will be considered a loss.

  • Replacements Received (Instock) - Use this status if the Supplier has sent you a replacement in return for the defective item. The inventory item will be placed back in Instock status.

  • Lost (Shrinkage) - Use this status if the item was lost in transit and will not be replaced. This will place the item into Shrinkage status and will be considered a loss.

Note: Our Knowledge Base Article, Inventory Status - What Does It Mean? has more details on each inventory status.

Reconcile RMA Items By Batch

1. On the RMA Tracking List page, change the filter status to RMA Sent and Apply. On the upper-right side of the page, click the Options (⋮) button. From the list of options, select View Batches.

2. On the RMA Batch List, find the batch that you would like to reconcile. Under the Actions column, click the Options (⋮) button. From the list of available actions, click View.

3. On the Batch Detail page, you will see a list of all items sent on this batch. Near the top-right of the screen, click Reconcile Items.

4. This will take you back to the RMA Tracking page with filtered results specifically for the single batch. Use the checkboxes to select the items you are ready to reconcile. Once you have made your selection, click the Edit Selected button. From the list of options, click Reconcile Selected.

5. A "Reconciled Selected" pop-up will appear. You will need to choose how these items should be reconciled. In the Movement field, select the appropriate status to move the item(s) based on the resolution the supplier has provided. Add any additional notes for the inventory items if necessary, and click Reconcile to submit and save your changes. This status will apply to all items that were selected.

  • RMA Accepted (RMA Credit) - Use this status if the Supplier has provided you with compensation (either a refund or credit) for the defective item.

  • RMA Rejected (Damaged) - Use this status if the Supplier has rejected the return. This will place the item into Damaged status and will be considered a loss.

  • Replacements Received (Instock) - Use this status if the Supplier has sent you a replacement in return for the defective item. The inventory item will be placed back in Instock status.

  • Lost (Shrinkage) - Use this status if the item was lost in transit and will not be replaced. This will place the item into Shrinkage status and will be considered a loss.

Note: Our Knowledge Base Article, Inventory Status - What Does It Mean? has more details on each inventory status.
