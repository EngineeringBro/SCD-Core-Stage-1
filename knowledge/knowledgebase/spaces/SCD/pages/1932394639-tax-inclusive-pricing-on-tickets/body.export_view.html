RepairQ’s tax-inclusive pricing feature is tailored for businesses operating in regions where local legislation mandates managing and displaying prices, including taxes.

In markets like Australia, where Goods and Services Tax (GST) applies, RepairQ can be configured to manage tax-inclusive pricing in compliance with regulations. This feature ensures that unit pricing, discounts, and ticket totals include tax, while accurately calculating the base price for gross profit analysis and providing a detailed tax breakdown for customer invoicing and accounting. The pricing labels can also be customized to default to the relevant 'tax-inclusive' pricing references within the ticket and lists.

Tax-Inclusive Pricing on Tickets

Ticket General Settings allow users to configure behaviors and appearance for tickets. If you are set up for tax-included pricing or follow GST guidelines, you can update the field names on your tickets to reflect your pricing structure. This will clarify information for your staff and customers. For more information, see Tax-inclusive Pricing: Customize Ticket Display.

The examples used below will include columns and fields that have been modified to align with GST standards.

Unit Price (Inc GST)

The Unit Price for each ticket item reflects the GST-inclusive value for the sale. The GST-exclusive value is rounded to the nearest cent.

  • An item on a ticket that shows a Unit Price (Inc GST) of $100.00. To find the actual price + tax of the item, you would use the equation:

    • Price (Ex GST) - $100 / 1.1 = $90.91 (price Ex GST, rounded up)

Qty / Hrs

The Qty/Hrs displayed for each ticket item typically represents the number of units. The exception is fixed labor items, which have an effective quantity of 1. For these items, the displayed value reflects the number of hours required to complete the repair, rounded to the nearest hundredth of an hour.

GST

The GST displayed for each ticket item represents the GST amount payable for the sale. The GST amount is rounded to the nearest cent.

  • The GST amount is calculated as: ([Unit Price Inc GST x Qty] - Discount In GST) / 1.1) * 0.1

    • GST - ($100.00 / 1.1) * 0.1 = $9.09

Line Total (Inc GST)

  • In the RepairQ User Interface, After Tax is Inc GST and Before Tax is Ex GST. The Line Total displayed for each ticket item represents the total amount payable for the sale.  

  • The Line Total amount is calculated as: (Unit Price (Inc GST) x Qty) - Discount (In GST) 

  • The equivalent Ex GST calculation is as follows: (Unit Price (Ex GST) x Qty) - Discount (Ex GST) + GST

Unit Price Editor

The Unit Price Editor allows users to select the Unit Price field and enter the Unit Price as the GST-inclusive (Inc GST) or GST-exclusive (Ex GST) amount.

Note: To use the Unit Price Editor, “Can Override Price” must be assigned to the Item Type or the Catalog Item.

  • After GST (selected) - The amount will be calculated as Unit Price (Inc GST). For an item entered at $100:

    • Price (Ex GST) - $100 / 1.1 = $90.91 (price Ex GST, rounded up)

    • GST - ($100.00 / 1.1) * 0.1 = $9.09

    • Line Total - $90.91 (Price Ex GST) + $9.09 (GST) = $100

  • After GST (not selected) - The amount will be calculated as Unit Price (Ex GST). For an item entered at $100:

    • Unit Price (With Adjusted GST) - $100 (price Ex GST) * 1.1 (GST) = $110

Discount (Inc GST)

The discount displayed for each ticket item reflects the GST-inclusive value for the sale. The GST-exclusive value is rounded to the nearest cent.

  • An item on a ticket that shows a Unit Price (Inc GST) of $100.00 with a discount of $10 (Inc GST). To find the actual price + tax of the item, you would use the following equations:

    • Price (Ex GST) - ( [$100 - $10] / 1.1 ) = $81.82 (rounded up)

    • GST - ( [$100 - $10] / 1.1 ) * 0.1 = $8.18

Discount Editor

The Discount Editor allows the user to click on the Discount field and enter the Discount as the GST-inclusive (Inc GST) or GST-exclusive (Ex GST) amount.

  • After GST (selected) - The amount will be calculated as Discount (Inc GST). For a discount of $10:

    • Price (Ex GST) - ( [$100 - $10] / 1.1 ) = $81.82 (rounded up)

    • GST - ( [$100 - $10] / 1.1 ) * 0.1 = $8.18

    • Line Total (Inc GST) - $81.82 + $8.18 = $90

  • After GST (not selected) - The amount will be calculated as Unit Price (Ex GST). For a discount entered at $10:

    • Unit Price (Inc GST) - $100

    • Discount (Adjusted for GST) - $10 * 1.1 = $11

    • Price (Ex GST) - ( [$100 - $11] / 1.1 ) = $80.91

    • GST - ( [$100 - $11] / 1.1] * .1) = $8.09

    • Line Total (Inc GST) - $80.91 + $8.09 = $89

Ticket-Level Discount

The Ticket Discount field allows the user to choose whether to apply a percentage or fixed amount discount for all items on the ticket. When applying a percentage discount, the default will treat the entered amount as Inc GST for locations that are configured for tax-inclusive pricing. When applying a fixed amount discount, the user will choose whether the amount is Inc GST or Ex GST.

Gross Profit Analysis (Ticket View)

When viewing the Gross Profit Analysis section on the Ticket view screen, it's important to note that all figures are calculated excluding taxes. This provides a clear view of your gross profit before any tax amounts are applied.

Special Considerations for Bundles

When bundling items on a ticket, the discount and taxable amounts are allocated across the parent and child items in the bundle. This distribution is essential because, although rare in Australia, a bundle can include taxable and non-taxable items or items with different tax rates and surcharge amounts. For more information, check out Tax Calculations for Bundled Items.

As a general rule, the total taxable amount for a bundle will never exceed the discounted sale price, even if the combined cost of all child items under the parent exceeds the bundle’s discounted sale price. For example, if you offer a $100 repair service and bundle three parts costing $50 each, the total taxable amount will still be capped at $100. For more information on tax settings on bundled items, see Setting up Taxes and Assigning Tax Rates to Items.
