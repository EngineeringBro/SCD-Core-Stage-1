RepairQ has introduced a new feature designed to enhance the security of your business and safeguard your customers' sensitive information. Our latest update allows Admins to automatically erase device passwords, from Repair Tickets once the ticket reaches Closed status. This proactive measure ensures that any customer information associated with the repair process is securely removed, providing added protection and peace of mind after the repair is complete. Key benefits include:

  • Improved Service Experience - Provides a secure and reliable service experience, fostering customer loyalty.

  • Privacy Protection and Compliance - Ensure personal device passwords are not stored on tickets, reducing the risk of exposing or misusing personal data, and following data protection regulations and industry standards.

Activate Password Auto-Erase

On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings. On the Settings Overview page, under the Tickets category, click General Settings.

On the Ticket Settings page, under the Automations section, activate the setting for Auto-Erase Device Passcode. Be sure to Save your settings.

Device Passwords on Repair Tickets

During a repair, the technician may need to access the phone’s operating system and apps to diagnose issues, back up data, and test the device after repair to ensure everything functions correctly. This information is collected during the device intake process. When viewing a Repair Ticket, the Password is visible to the user.

When the Auto-Erase feature is active, the Password information is deleted from the system when a user progresses the ticket to Closed status. For locations processing claims, the password is deleted when progressing to the Device Returned step.

Note: The device history is still retained for the customer, however, if this device is added to a repair ticket in the future, you will need to capture the password again.
