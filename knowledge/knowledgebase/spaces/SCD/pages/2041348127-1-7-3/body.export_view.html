Welcome to RepairQ 1.7.3! This release features Return Repairs, helping you more accurately process and track repairs returned for reworks. Inventory Count Improvements will speed up the process of assigning counts to staff and reconciling counts when they're done. In addition, we’ve made improvements to customer searches.

See the new features in our 1.7.3 Walkthrough Video

Return Repairs

Return Repair allows you to track return repairs (reworks) covered by your service warranty. Return Repair tickets are linked to the original repair ticket, making it easy to track the repaired device(s) repair history. This feature provides reports revealing how much reworks are costing your store and which technicians performed the repairs leading to the reworks. Return Repairs will help you understand your losses due to reworks and what is causing them. This will help you make the necessary changes to reduce rework occurrences.

Inventory Count Efficiency

Counting inventory just got easier. You are now able to assign a user multiple pages of items to count with the added “Assign All” action. In addition, you can reconcile all counts resulting in a zero variance with one click. For your convenience, we also added a “Cancel All” action which will delete all uncounted assignments.

Customer Search Improvements

1.7.3 makes improvements to the customer search capability. You now have the ability to search for customers by Customer Segment, and by Customer Referral. Using these search tools, you keep track of customer acquisition for each group of customers, and more easily segment them into separate lists.

Bug Fixes

  1. Inventory valuation reports now use inventory item status updated date, instead of the simple updated on date.

  2. Added RMA Credit to the list of statuses excluded from average cost changes. Restoring average cost for any historical RMA Credit items is available by request.

  3. Fixed a few issues related to editing staff timesheets and matching staff with their assigned managers.
