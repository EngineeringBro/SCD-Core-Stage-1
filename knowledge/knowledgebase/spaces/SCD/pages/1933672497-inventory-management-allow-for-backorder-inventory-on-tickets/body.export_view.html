By default, an inventory item with no stock availability cannot be added to a ticket. This is to ensure that your location cannot oversell your inventory to customers. Admins can allow for backorder items at the company or level. If a user adds an inventory item that is not in stock to a ticket and update/save the ticket status, the system will create a new, placeholder entry into your inventory with Back Ordered status. A ticket with a backorder item cannot be closed until you have fulfilled the order.

Backorder items can be fulfilled manually or automatically through a Purchase Order (PO) replenishment. If the same item has been back ordered on multiple tickets, the PO will fulfill older tickets first.

RepairQ includes features to notify managers on the dashboard when backorder items are added to tickets, and the PO also includes reordering notifications, to help you replenish these orders first.

Authorize Your Location to Create Backorder Items on Tickets

On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Location Settings. On the Settings Overview page, under the Tickets section, click General Settings.

Note: This setting can also be authorized at the Company (Global) level by going to Company Settings.

Ticket General Settings

On the General Settings page, toggle the setting for Auto-backorder out-of-stock items to ON and Save your settings.

When this setting is activated, you can add out-of-stock items as Back Ordered on tickets. This item must be replenished before the ticket can be closed.

Ticket Status Advancement for Backorder Items

You can configure how tickets advance between statuses based on whether a backordered item is present. In Location Settings → Ticket General Settings, you can restrict ticket progression to certain statuses or limit it by ticket type. These settings help control workflow by preventing tickets with backordered items from advancing until the necessary stock is available.

  • Do Not Allow On – Enables or disables the restriction.

  • Ticket Statuses – Specifies which statuses cannot be selected if the ticket contains a backordered item. By default, you cannot close a ticket with a backorder item.

  • Ticket Types – Specifies which ticket types cannot be saved if a backordered item is present.
