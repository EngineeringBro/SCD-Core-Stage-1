As a rule of thumb, marking a catalog item as inactive will not void any inventory associated with that catalog entry. When checking the Inventory Valuation Report, you may see that there are inventory items that are bloating your inventory because they are linked to a catalog item that has been previously deactivated/retired. Users can bulk-edit these items out of inventory if they no longer exist.

Finding Active Inventory with Inactive Catalog Entries

1. On the Navigation Bar, go to Inventory → Inventory Detail.

2. On the Inventory Detail screen, use the filters to set Inventory Focus: Inactive Only and Date Added: All Time. Next, click Apply to filter results.

3. Set another filter for Status: Select All then edit the filter to remove the following consumed inventory statuses:

  • Damaged

  • Sold

  • Voided

  • Parts Only

  • Shrinkage

  • RMA Credit

Your list should only include active inventory statuses, and will look similar to this:

4. After you have updated the filter, click Apply to submit the new changes.

5. Any items that match your filters will appear on the Inventory Detail list.

Bulk-Edit Items Out of Inventory

1. Select items by clicking their checkbox on the left side of the listed item.

2. After you have made your selection(s), click the Options button on the upper-right of the page. From the list of options, click Edit Selected.

3. A Bulk Edit pop-up window will appear. You can move these out of inventory by selecting Attribute: Status and New value: Voided (Or select any other consumed inventory status that may better fit the status of these items). Add any additional notes for your records, and make sure to click Apply to save these changes.

This will consume these items out of your inventory and remove them from your inventory valuation reports.
