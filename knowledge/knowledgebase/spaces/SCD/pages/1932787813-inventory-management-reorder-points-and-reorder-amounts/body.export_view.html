Effective inventory management is crucial for maintaining optimal stock levels and ensuring smooth operations. RepairQ allows users to monitor inventory through features like Reorder Points (ROPs) and Reorder Amounts (ROAs). These tools provide visual cues and notifications, helping managers identify when stock levels fall below designated thresholds. By proactively managing inventory, businesses can minimize shortages, avoid overstocking, and streamline procurement workflows.

Users assigned the Inventory Manager role or higher can set Reorder Points (ROPs) and Reorder Amounts (ROAs) for catalog items at their assigned location(s). Additionally, Admins can configure these settings at the Company (global) level.

What are Reorder Points and Reorder Amounts?

  • Reorder Points (ROPs) - These thresholds notify managers when an item's in-stock quantity falls below a specified level. Visual cues on the Inventory Summary page indicate quantity status, while notifications on the Dashboard and during the Purchase Order process ensure inventory needs are addressed promptly.

  • Reorder Amounts (ROAs) - Simplify ordering by assigning a default quantity to reorder for each item. When adding an item to a Purchase Order, the ROA is automatically populated, streamlining the procurement workflow.

How Reorder Point Notifications Work

Reorder Point notifications account for the total of in-stock and ordered items.

  • Instock - Items in this status are available for use and are ready to be added to a ticket.

  • Ordered - Items in this status are pending delivery and will soon be added to inventory, so they are counted towards the available quantity.

  • Pulled - Items in this status are pending sale and are not counted as Instock inventory, so they subtract from the available quantity.

Dashboard Alert

The Dashboard notifies you when items have fallen below their ROP and offers links to view the items on the Inventory Summary screen or create a new PO to fulfill the items.

Purchase Order Reminder

When creating a PO, the “Reorder Required” tab lists all items below their ROP with additional details, including Reorder Amounts and quick links to add them to the order.

Inventory Summary Screen

RepairQ will maintain a “good” status (green) if the combined total of in-stock and ordered quantities exceeds the assigned ROP. If the total meets the assigned ROP, RepairQ will maintain a "warning” status (yellow). If the total falls below the assigned ROP, the item will be flagged with a "reorder" status (red).

Assign Reorder Points and Reorder Amounts

On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Catalog. On the Catalog Item List page, use the filters to find the item you would like to update ROP/ROA information for.

Adjust ROP/ROA at the Global Level (All Locations)

After locating the item, click the Options button next to the line item, under the Actions column. From the list of available options, click Edit.

On the Catalog Item Edit page, locate the Behaviors section. Under this section, update the Reorder Point and Reorder Amount fields to your preference. Be sure to save your changes before leaving the page.

Adjust ROP/ROA at the Location Level (Location Override)

After locating the item, click the Options button next to the line item, under the Actions column. From the list of available options, click Location Override.

On the Location Override pop-up, you can see the default amounts at the global level. Update the Reorder Point and Reorder Amount fields for your location to your preference. When you are finished, click Save Changes to update the totals.
