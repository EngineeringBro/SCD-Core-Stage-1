The Qsite Ticket Approvals feature helps you request and record consumer consent for a repair. You can configure your requirements and then request the customer's consent prior to or during the repair. Approval requests can be sent out when a ticket is placed into your status of choice, and you can customize how you want to deliver the request to the customer. Consumers can view, make notes, and accept or reject your request for approval. Once accepted, your ticket status will update, allowing your staff to continue with approved repairs.

Once all configurations are set, you can create a ticket and advance it to the respective status. This article will outline the approval process from the customer's perspective.

Sending an Approval Request to a Customer

Once all configuration is set you can begin the approval process by saving the ticket in Pending Approval status. When the RepairQ user edits the ticket, they will be prompted to add any additional notes and create the Approval. The ticket will be locked from editing until the customer approves the estimate/quote.

Customer Notification and Response

The customer will receive an email with a link to the approval request. The customer can view the general details of the ticket and Reject or Approve the request.

The customer will see a confirmation screen showing the results of their approval, and the approval/rejection notification will be sent to the designated email in the automated notification.

Note: If you have required the customer to view details before approving, the initial Approve button will be replaced with Next, and clicking that button will take them to a screen to view all details before approving.

Manually Approving/Rejecting a Request

If the customer does not have access to their email or did not receive the request, they can contact you to approve/reject the request. When viewing the ticket you can click the Submit button (thumbs-up icon) under the Approvals section of the ticket or edit the ticket to view the manual approval screen.

Note: A pending approval can be deleted by clicking the Cancel button under the Actions column.

Here, you can Accept or Reject the approval. By default, the Approved by customer field is checked, since it is understood the customer has given their consent. If you uncheck this field, the customer's information will be available for you to contact them.

Note: We encourage entering a note for record-keeping purposes before accepting/rejecting a request.

Approval Post-Submission

Once the Approval has been completed, the ticket will be updated with the customer’s response. At this point, you may proceed with the ticket.

Note: If you manually recorded the incorrect response, you can click the Add button to send another approval and submit a new response.
