Offering trade-in options to your customers helps your store open new revenue streams through refurbished sales. It can boost customer loyalty, increase the sales of new devices, and will reduce your environmental footprint by recycling previously owned devices. Customers who trade in their old devices can even apply that money/credit towards a new purchase from your store. When a Trade-in ticket is completed, any traded items will automatically become inventory items for resale or for use as parts.

Create a New Trade-in Ticket

On the top Navigation Bar, hover on the Tickets category then select + Tradein.

(Optional) Under the Customer & Billing Agent section, click the Attach Customer button search for an existing customer to attach to the ticket or create a new customer. We recommend linking the ticket to a customer in case you need to easily look up the ticket in the future, but it isn’t a requirement for Trade-in tickets.

Under the Trade-In Items section of the ticket, use the Smart Search box to search for your catalog for a corresponding inventory item. Alternatively, click the Look Up button to search for items based on Item Type, Manufacturer, and name.

Note: If you do not have a corresponding item in your inventory, you will need to create the Catalog Item first. Users can create items without leaving the ticket flow if Catalog Quick Create is activated for Trade-in tickets. The Catalog Item must be marked as Inventoried and for it to appear as a Trade-in item.

When adding a trade-in item, a pop-up screen will appear. Confirm that the device details are correct, then enter the required and optional fields. After entering the information, click Add Item.

  • Serial Number - Enter the serial number/IMEI for the device, based on your record-keeping preference. The system will check if the item already exists in your inventory.

  • Paymout Amount - Enter the amount you are paying the customer for the item.

  • Inventory Status - Select the inventory status the item will be placed in upon finalizing the ticket.

  • Carrier - (Optional) Select the mobile carrier for the device.

  • Inventory Notes - (Optional) Add any additional notes for the inventory item.

  • Diagnostic Notes/Complaint - Add any additional notes about the functionality and condition of the device.

After adding the trade-in item, the balance due on the ticket will update to reflect the payout amount to the customer. Optionally, if the customer is making a purchase during this transaction, you can add items under the Ticket Items section of the ticket.

To close the ticket, the remaining balance must be zero. Use the Cash Transaction shortcut or select an alternative paymout method by clicking Add Transaction.

Note: The available transaction payment methods are based on the methods you selected when opening the VCT.

Once you have balanced the ticket, click the Close button to finish the transaction. The trade-in item(s) will be moved into the inventory status that you selected during intake. RepairQ also creates a Refurb Ticket for each device added, so you can keep a record of the Refurbishment Process.

(Optional) After closing the ticket, you can print a label for the trade-in device(s) from the ticket. For more information on this process, see our article, Printing a Ticket Label.
