Lists are powerful tools throughout RepairQ, offering features like filters, keyword search, and saving favorite list views for future use. Users can customize which columns of data are visible, ensuring that key information is readily accessible when viewing and sorting lists. This flexibility extends to exporting data, allowing users to tailor the information to their needs. Customized list views can be saved and shared with staff, enhancing convenience for everyone.

  • Data Management - Quickly filter, search, and access relevant information in your lists.

  • Personalization - Customize visible columns to focus on the most important data.

  • Collaboration - Share customized list views with your staff, ensuring consistent access to key information.

Customize List Columns

Navigate to any List you would like to customize. For this example, we will modify the Inventory Summary List View. On the Navigation Bar, go to Inventory → Inventory Summary.

On the Inventory Summary screen, you can see the default columns along the top row of the list. To edit your available columns, click the Options button (⋮) on the right side of the page. From the dropdown list of options, select Edit Columns.

Add or Remove Data Columns

Once in Column Edit mode, click the Add Button (+), and the Column Edit window will appear. The Selected Columns section includes the columns that are currently on your List.

Scroll down until you reach the Add Columns section. This section contains all the fields you can add to your Inventory Detail view. Select the checkbox next to the field(s) you wish to add to the Column List. Alternatively, you can search for the desired column(s) by typing a keyword into the category box. The pop-up will then display any columns containing that keyword.

To remove columns, uncheck the box next to the field.

Once your list is customized, click the Finish button (checkmark) to update your list. After updating, your list will reflect the new columns.

Change Column Order

You can use the Edit Columns feature to reorder your columns. Click and drag each category to your desired order. Once you have changed the order to your preference, click the Finish button (checkmark) to update your list.

Your List will now show as Default-Modified. If you want to save or share the new List View, check out our Knowledge Base Article, Saving and Sharing Custom List Views.

If you reset the filters, your list will revert to the Default view.
