The Catalog lists all items and services available for your store. Any item that can be purchased, sold, repaired, or used as parts needs a corresponding inventory item. Services and Labor can be added to the Catalog to charge customers for services and report the Cost Of Goods Sold (COGS) on repairs.

The field details for adding a Catalog Item may vary, depending on your setup. For a detailed explanation of these fields, check out our RepairQ Catalog Overview.

A user with the Inventory Manager role or higher can create catalog items.

Add a New Catalog Item

On the RepairQ Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Catalog. When viewing the Catalog List, click the Add Button (+) on the upper-right of the page.

Enter all applicable naming details for your Catalog Item at your discretion. The required fields are highlighted below. After filling out the item attributes, click Save on the upper-right side of the screen to submit your changes.

Note: Various fields in the Details area allow you to add alternative searchable names, Stock Keeping Units (SKUs), and Universal Product Codes (UPCs) to enable barcode scanning.

Required Fields

  • Name - This must be a unique name for your item.

  • Item Type -This is used to categorize your Catalog Items for Taxes, Cost of Goods, and other reporting.

  • Manufacturer - This is an additional level of categorization for Inventory Items.

Optional Fields

These fields are important for maintaining your inventory. Once a Catalog Item has associated inventory or ticket history, some attributes will be locked from changes. This is to ensure accurate reporting and maintain data integrity.

  • Cost - This is the cost of the item from the Supplier. This is used for COGS reporting.

  • Price, excl. tax - This is the retail price of the item, without taxes. This is the amount the customer will be charged for the item/service.

    • If your location utilizes tax-inclusive pricing, this field will update to Price Inc. tax.

  • Repairable - Check this box to allow the Catalog Item to be added as the Repair Device on Repair Tickets.

  • Inventoried - Check this box to record a physical quantity of this item in your inventory for record-keeping.

  • Serialized - Check this box to require a serial number when an item is added to inventory or added to a ticket. This option appears when an item is marked as Inventoried.

  • Default Qty/Hrs - Enter a default quantity amount when adding an item to a ticket. Use decimals for partial labor hours.

  • Active - Active items can be added to tickets and, if inventoried, can hold a stock amount. When inactive, the item is removed from searches and lists. You can filter your catalog for inactive items and reactivate them as needed.

After filling out the item attributes, click Save in the upper-right corner of the screen to submit your changes. The new item will then appear in the Catalog List, where you can filter by item name, creation date, and other attributes.

Edit a Catalog Item

You can edit Catalog Items when you need to update details. Once a Catalog Item has associated inventory or ticket history, some attributes will be locked from changes.

Navigate to your Catalog and filter for the item you wish to edit. Click the Options button on the right side of the item to expand additional actions and click Edit. Once you’ve updated the fields and selections as needed, click Save on the upper-right side of the page to submit your changes.
