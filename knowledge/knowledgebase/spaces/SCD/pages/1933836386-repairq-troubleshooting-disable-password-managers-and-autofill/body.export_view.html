Because RepairQ is cloud-based, we highly recommend disabling your browser password manager when using our application. Leaving a password manager active can cause security risks, including saving the manager's (or another employee's) login details, which could be used for fraudulent use. Auto-fill can interfere with field details in RepairQ settings as well as credit card swipes during the transaction process.

We have included instructions on how to turn off these features on our commonly used browsers. Click the link below to jump to the related browser settings.

  • Google Chrome Browser

  • Firefox Browser

  • Safari Browser

  • Microsoft Edge Browser

Note: We also recommend turning off third-party password managers like LastPass, etc., as these applications have similar effects on browser fields.

Google Chrome Browser

1. On the upper-right corner of the browser, window, click the Menu button (⋮). From the drop-down list of options, click Settings.

2. On the Settings screen, select Auto-fill from the left column. From the Auto-fill settings, select Password Manager.

3. On the Password Manager screen, turn OFF the options for Offer to save passwords and Auto Sign-in. Then click the Back arrow to go back to Auto-fill settings.

4. Repeat the same process for Payment Methods and Addresses and more.

Firefox Browser

1. On the upper-right corner of the browser, window, click the Menu button. From the drop-down list of options, click Settings.

2. On the Settings screen, select Privacy & Security from the left column. Scroll down until you get to the Logins and Passwords section and uncheck the selections for Ask to save logins and passwords for websites and Autofill logins and passwords.

3. Scroll down to Forms and Autofill and uncheck Autofill addresses and Autofill credit cards.

Safari Browser

1. On the upper-left corner of the browser window, click Safari. From the dropdown options, click Settings.

2. In the Settings window, click the Autofill tab. Under the AutoFill web forms section, uncheck the autofill details.

Microsoft Edge Browser

1. On the upper-right corner of the browser window, click the Menu button. From the drop-down list of options, click Settings.

2. On the Settings page, click Profiles on the left column. Under the Your Profile section, click Personal Info.

3. On the Personal Info page, toggle OFF the selections for Save and fill basic info, Automatically fill my info on sign up forms, and Save and fill custom info. Then click the Back arrow to go back to the Profile settings.

4. On the Your Profile page, click Passwords.

5. On the Passwords page, Toggle OFF the selections for Offer to save passwords, Automatically save passwords, and Autofill passwords.
