Released 08/29/2017 12:41 CT (17:42 UTC)

PAX Payments and Ticket Auto-save

RepairQ auto-saves the ticket when a payment is processed on the PAX device. If you process a payment on the PAX device when the ticket is not save-able (e.g. missing required information), then RepairQ saves the payment onto the ticket for you. If you are working with a new ticket, then a ticket in the "new/draft" status is saved for you with the payment and the customer attached so that even if you abandon the ticket before correcting the errors and re-saving, you retain the record of the payment in RepairQ. If you process multiple credit card payments on a ticket while the ticket is not save-able, then it could result in duplicate tickets.

We have resolved the issue that allowed these duplicate tickets to be created.

Signed Custom Form Downloads

We fixed an issue that would prevent some forms from being download-able due to a permissions error.

Ticket Notification Buttons

If the customer is opted out of notifications, notifications to the customer will not be sent, but the ticket email & sms buttons gave the impression that notifications could still be sent to the customer. We have updated the feedback messaging to clearly indicate when messages are not created due to notification preferences or invalid contact info.

Ticket Defective / Damaged Notes

The notes from the defective / damaged window on tickets were not being saved on the inventory item record. Now the notes save on the inventory item.
