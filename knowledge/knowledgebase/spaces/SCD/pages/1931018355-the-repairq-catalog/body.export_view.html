Your Catalog is a comprehensive list of all the products, items, and services you offer to customers. It includes details like product names, descriptions, prices, and other relevant information. Maintaining an inventory catalog has several benefits:

  • Organization - Keep track of all the items in your store, making it easier to find and manage your stock.

  • Accurate Pricing - Having a detailed catalog ensures you have consistent and up-to-date prices for each item, reducing the risk of pricing errors.

  • Inventory Management - Your catalog is linked to your inventory, allowing you to monitor stock levels in real-time. This helps prevent overstocking or running out of essential items.

  • Sales Tracking, Reporting, and Analytics - With a well-maintained catalog, you can easily track sales and pinpoint sales trends. This helps you to identify popular products and make data-driven decisions to optimize your business.

Setting up Your Catalog

The Catalog is closely tied to the accuracy of inventory management and reports, so we have designed it to focus on structure and consistency. Creating a structured catalog reduces the likelihood of typos, duplicate entries, incorrectly named items, etc. Features like Catalog Quick Create allow you to grow and refine your catalog so you can stay up-to-date with new products and services. When setting up your new RepairQ environment, be prepared to spend time developing your Catalog beforehand, so you can effectively use RepairQ; it’s well worth the investment! As a result, you’ll have good business data that can generate clear and accurate reports for the metrics you want to see.

Item Types

Item Types are high-level categories to organize your catalog. This allows you to assign specific attributes to the entire group of catalog items under these types. Item Types also dictate assigned tax rates, price override allowances, identify whether items are parts or labor, establish parent/child relationships for bundling, and more. Before building your Catalog, it’s a good idea to decide what Item Types you need to set up first. For more information on this, see our article, Item Types - Overview.

When establishing different Item Types, you might start by asking yourself the following questions:

  • How many categories do I need to organize my catalog and physical inventory? - This varies based on your business and which products and services you offer. These are based on your preference.

  • What high-level categories do I need to create to track my metrics? - This is related to the organization of your catalog. Since some reports are organized by Item Type, categorizing items under various item types allows for more specific targeting when filtering reports.

  • What are the sales tax/value-added tax laws in my state? - These vary based on the transaction, and affect whether items are taxable, pre-taxed, or if you decide to bundle parts & labor or charge for these items, separately. Make sure you consider the following categories for your catalog:

    • Merchandise

    • Parts

    • Labor/Services

    • Parts bundled to labor as a single line-item charge

    • Labor bundled to parts as a single line-item charge

  • Do I want to bundle parts & labor together into a single line-item charge on tickets, or do I want to charge for them separately? - Bundling offers simplified pricing for your repair services by absorbing the price of consumed parts into the price of the repair. For more information on this, see Catalog Items: Bundle Relationships, Auto-Bundling, and Auto-Adding Items.

  • Do I want to create generic catalog items used as placeholders in repair tickets or mark all sellable devices as repairable? For more information, see Catalog Items: Creating Sellable, Repairable, and Trade-in Devices.

After deciding on an initial list of Item Types, admins have access to create new types for your catalog. Some examples include:

  • Device - Phone

  • Accessory

  • Part - Phone

  • Service - Hourly

  • Other Device(s) - Tablet, Game Console, 2-Way Radio, etc.

Manufacturers

Manufacturers add an additional level of categorization for inventory items. RepairQ offers a pre-filled list of manufacturers, but you can also add/customize these as your catalog grows over time. For more information on this process, see our article, Creating a New Manufacturer. Here are a few tips when creating new manufacturers for your catalog.

  • You can add a "N/A" or "DNA" for catalog items where "manufacturer" doesn't apply.

  • You can add a manufacturer called "Internal Labor" for labor Catalog Items.

Adding Catalog Items

Once you have your Item Types and Manufacturers set up, you can begin building your catalog. We recommend using standardized naming conventions for your Catalog Items. This allows you to grow your business without losing the ability to track important metrics. Here are a few guidelines to help with this process:

  • Create a catalog item for each distinct item you want to add to a ticket. If you are adding a large volume of items to your catalog, see our Catalog Import section for more details on streamlining this process.

  • Create a catalog item for each item you want to track separately. Many reports are organized by catalog item, including Revenue, Productivity, and Marketing reports. See Catalog Items: Creating Sellable, Repairable, and Trade-in Devices for more information.

  • It is acceptable to create separate Catalog Items for devices that are both Inventory Items (things you sell) and Repair Devices (things you repair). In fact, we recommend this process. Catalog items used specifically as repair devices can be more generic in their description since certain attributes of the repair device can be entered on the ticket.

    • Marking a repair item “Is Repairable” ensures that the item can be added to Repair tickets. You may want to do this for repair devices but leave this field unchecked for sellable items.

    • Use a generic name that includes the brand and model for repair devices. For example, “iPhone 15 Pro.” When you add the item to a Repair ticket, you can add the color, memory, etc. to the description.

    • Use a specific, descriptive name for items that you want to sell. For example, “iPhone 15 Pro 256GB Blue Titanium.” This is a device that can be tracked specifically in your inventory.

    • With so many models of available devices, you may see occasional devices you don’t normally repair or sell in your store. For these, we recommend using the Catalog Quick Create feature to create items on-the-fly, rather than leaving the ticket screen. Make sure you communicate your preferred naming methods to your staff when activating this feature to ensure that your catalog remains consistent.

Catalog Item Attributes

The list below gives an explanation of the available fields and selections when creating a new Catalog Item.

Primary Details

  • Name - This is the primary name to identify the Catalog Item. This must be unique for each item.

  • Friendly Name - This is an alternative, customer-friendly name shown on receipts.

  • Short Name - This is an alternative name for the item as it appears on an Item Grid.

  • Item Type - This is a classification of the catalog item. This must match an existing Item Type in your Catalog settings.

  • Manufacturer - This is a secondary classification of the catalog that notes the manufacturer of the item. This must match an existing Manufacturer in your Catalog Settings

    • You can add a Manufacturer name of “N/A” for catalog items where this doesn't apply.

    • You can add a Manufacturer called "Internal Labor" for labor catalog items

  • SKU - Stock Keeping Unit. Similar to a UPC, this unique identifier offers a more detailed identification than barcodes. SKUs can include product details like size, color, and style, providing additional inventory info.

  • UPC - Universal Product Code. This unique identifier is used to track stock and inventory of your items. 

  • Search Key - Additional searchable keywords/tags for the Catalog Item. This can be used when searching your inventory page, for an item to add to a ticket, etc.

    • Multiple search keys for a catalog item must be listed on separate lines.

  • Bin - This is generally used for inventory organization. If you plan to keep this item in a designated area (bin), you can label that location with this field.

  • Note - If you have an additional note for this Catalog Item, you can add it in this field.

Pricing

  • Cost - This is the expense of your business to produce, acquire, or deliver that item. This may be affected by factors like your purchase price of the item, materials, labor, overhead, and other associated costs.

    • If a cost is entered for the Catalog Item, it will automatically be applied when adding an item to your inventory.

  • Price - This is the retail price of the item. This is the amount that the customer pays to purchase the item. This may be affected by factors like profit margin, market demand, etc.

    • If a price is entered for the Catalog Item, it will automatically be applied when adding the item to a ticket or into inventory.

  • Advertised Price - By default, the “Price” field is used for standard retail pricing. If the price of the item varies when advertised on Qsite, that price can be entered here. If the “Price Advertised” box is checked, but nothing is entered here, the Price will be displayed instead.

  • Allow Cost Override - This allows you to change the cost of the item when added to a ticket.

  • Allow Supplier Override - This allows you to change the item's supplier when added to a ticket.

  • Price Advertised - Select whether to show or hide the Price or Advertised Price on Qsite.

    • When selected, the price displayed on Qsite will reflect the price entered in the “Advertised Price” field. If that field is left blank, the Price will be displayed instead.

  • Can Override Price - This allows you to change the cost of the item when added to a ticket

Behaviors

  • Repairable - This will allow you to attach this Catalog item as a "repair device" on a repair ticket.

  • Inventoried - Items marked as inventoried have a quantity that can be tracked in RepairQ. These items need to be stocked in to be used on tickets and can be replenished via Purchase Orders, inventory adjustments, and Inventory Counts. Non-inventoried items can be added to tickets however, there is no way to track the amount of this item in your possession.

  • Default Qty/Hours - This allows you to assign a default quantity/labor hour amount when adding the item to a ticket. The most common entry for item quantity is "1", and you can enter decimals for partial hours for labor items.

  • Advertised - Select this for the item to be shown as an available item/service on Qsite.

  • Only For Service Programs - Selecting this option will flag the item for use only when working on a Service Program Repair Ticket.

  • Active - If checked, this will be active and searchable in the catalog. Uncheck to retire an item as inactive.

  • Royalty Percent - If you have a franchise agreement and need to pay a percentage of your revenue to the rights holder, you can set that amount at the Location, Item Type, or Catalog Item level. Setting a royalty percent at the Catalog Item level will override royalty settings in the other fields. It will inherit what is entered at the higher level if left blank.

  • Flag the Ticket Item as Royalty Exempt - When this is selected for a catalog item, it will be exempt from reporting a royalty percentage when added to a ticket.

  • Flag the Entire Ticket as Royalty Exempt - When this is selected for a catalog item, adding this item to a ticket will exempt all items on that ticket from reporting royalties.

Locations

Select whether the item will be created as a Global item or a Location-only item. Global items will appear in the catalog for all locations, while Location-only items will only appear in the catalog at the location where they were created.

Journal Entry Classification

If this item requires different accounts than what you have configured for your Journal Entry Classifications, you can specify them here.

OEM Program

If using Apple IRP or Samsung GSPN service programs, this allows you to designate the item for use with a specific program

Visual Identifications

  • Has QR Code - This is only applicable to the Samsung GSPN service program. This allows you to scan the QR code information on replacement parts during the claim process.

Catalog Import

RepairQ offers convenient inventory management by allowing users to add and update Catalog Items via an import file. Users can effortlessly create catalog items, registering the items and services in your store. This process is streamlined through import files, supporting .xlsx (Excel), .csv (comma-separated values), and .txt (tab-separated values), with .xlsx recommended for the best experience. Before importing your catalog, there are a few things you should remember:

  • Item Types used in the import file must already exist in your system. See Creating a New Item Type for more information on this process.

  • Manufacturers used in the import file must already exist in your system. See Creating a New Manufacturer for more information on this process.

  • Catalog Item Names, SKUs, and UPCs must be unique in order to be accepted. Items cannot share these attributes.

  • See Catalog Management: Importing Catalog Items for instructions on the import process.
