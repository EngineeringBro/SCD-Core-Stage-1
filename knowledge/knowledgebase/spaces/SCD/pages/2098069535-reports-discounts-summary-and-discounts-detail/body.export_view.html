The Discount Report shows all discounts applied to your ticket items. We recommend accounting based on closed tickets only; once a ticket is closed the date will no longer change, and the sales are reported as revenue. By default, filters are set to closed tickets, because it is the most reliable method for this business model. For further information about accrual-based reporting, see our article, RepairQ Accounting Principles.

View Discount Reporting

On the Navigation Bar, click Reports. On the Reports page, click the Returns & Adjustments dropdown to expand available reports. From the list click Discounts Summary or Discounts Detail.

Use the available filters to scope results based on the assigned parameters. Once you have made your selections, click Apply Filters to run the report.

  • Date Range - Select a date range for your scope of results.

  • Ticket Status - Choose to view results based on ticket status. By default, the report will run for tickets in Closed status, since ticket information is finalized.

  • Location - Select a specific location or an aggregate report for multiple/all locations.

  • Discount - Select a specific discount or an aggregate report for multiple/all discounts applied on a ticket.

Exporting the Report

On the upper-right corner of the page, you can switch between Summary and Detail reporting, Export to Excel, and Hide/Show Filters from the report view. The export will include data matching your filtered results.

Discount Summary

The Discount Summary Report gives you a broad summary of discounts applied to each Item Type. This report also includes total discounts for each type, discounts returned, and net discounts.

Discount Detail

The Discount Detail Report list tickets with columns that detail Date/Time, Catalog Item, Discount Name, and sales details. There are also links to view the ticket and Inventory Item Summary.
