RepairQ offers the convenience of Quick Sale tickets, empowering users to handle various transactions. Whether a customer is purchasing devices and accessories or paying for services, Quick Sale tickets are designed for efficiency and simplicity, allowing you to serve customers swiftly without a complex process. With Quick Sale, you can easily process transactions, reducing wait times and enhancing the overall shopping experience for your customers. Various benefits include:

  • Simplicity - Simplify the sales process with a straightforward interface for creating and processing transactions.

  • Seamless Payment Integration - Enjoy seamless integration with leading payment providers such as Bluefin and Square, allowing for secure and efficient processing of transactions directly within tickets.

  • Accurate Reporting - Generate precise sales and inventory reports with each Quick Sale transaction, providing valuable, real-time insights into product performance and stock levels.

Creating a new Quick Sale Ticket

You can find the Quick Sale button under the Tickets section of the Navigation Bar or while viewing a customer's profile.

Note: You will need to assign a VCT before you can accept payments on the ticket.

Log into your RepairQ Location. On the Navigation Bar, go to Tickets → +Quick Sale.

Note: Alternatively, you can initiate a Quick Sale ticket directly from a Customer Profile. The customer information will automatically be added to the ticket when it is created.

Under the Customer & Billing Agent section, you have the option to have no customer assigned to the ticket (Anonymous) or you can click Attach Customer to search for and add an existing Customer Profile or create a new profile from the ticket. We recommend attaching a customer for record-keeping purposes.

Note: An anonymous ticket cannot be saved in any status other than Closed. If you need to save the ticket for later, you will need to assign a customer to the ticket.

When attaching a customer, the search field can look for customers by name, phone number, email address, or customer loyalty ID.

Under the Ticket Items section, you have the option to add an item with the Search Box by searching your inventory by keyword, UPC, SKU, or serial number. Alternatively, you can also click click Look up to perform an advanced search of our inventory by category.

Add Items from Catalog Search Box

Scan or search for items by keyword, serial number, IMEI, or additional fields assigned to your catalog items, e.g. SKU or UPC. Searching for an item will show the in-stock quantity of an item, the price, and additional identifiers for that item. Clicking the item will add it to the ticket. If you are adding a serialized item, you will need to enter the serial number/IMEI for the device that will be sold to the customer.

Look Up a Catalog Item / Add Bulk Inventory Items

Perform an advanced search based on the assigned Item Type and Catalog Item name or, alternatively, select an item from the Item Grid, if it has been set up. You can also use this pop-up to sell multiple, non-serialized items by using the Quantity/Hours field to add items in bulk. Once you have made your selection, click Add Item to add it to the ticket.

Note: To avoid the risk of browser timeout when saving the ticket, we recommend a 75 Line-item limit for Ticket Items. When saving, please be patient until the page refreshes. Manually refreshing the page may cause ticket errors.

Once you have added an item, it will be visible under the Ticket Items section. There are a few actions that can be taken after an item is added to a ticket.

  • Note - Add notes to the item that can be accessed later. e.g., "Gave 10% discount due to damaged packaging."

  • Relations - Assign a staff member who will receive credit for the sale of the item. For more information, see Assigning Ticket Items to Users.

  • Unit Price - Most parts and accessories will have their price displayed here. This field may be editable, based on the attributes set for that item in the Catalog.

  • Qty/Hrs - If this is an inventory item, this will display the quantity. If this is a labor item, the labor hours need to be tracked in this field to ensure proper reporting.

  • Discount/Ticket Discount - You can apply a discount set by percentage or dollar amount. Entering an amount in the Ticket Discount field (located above the list of ticket items) will discount every item in the ticket by that percentage/dollar amount.

  • Status - Generally, this will be in Pulled or Sold status.

  • Damaged/Defective Item - (Thumb-down icon) This will allow the user to mark the item as Defective (to initiate the RMA process) or as Damaged (to report a loss due to unusable status).

  • Waive Charge - (Currency icon) This will allow the user to waive all charges for the line item. This information can be tracked in the Waived Charges Report.

  • Override Item - (Asterisk icon) This will allow the user to override the Taxable Amount, Cost (if allowed at the Catalog item level), and/or the Supplier.

  • Remove Item - Removes the item from the ticket and places it back into stock if the item is inventoried. 

After you add an item to a ticket, the Totals section at the bottom-right of the screen will show an amount due for the customer.

Accepting Payment

You will need to assign a VCT before you can accept payments on the ticket.

Under the Transactions section of the ticket, you can record payments taken from the customer to balance the amount due. 

Cash Shortcut - If the customer is paying with cash, select a pre-set cash amount based on bills or enter an exact amount paid.

Request Payment - If using an Integrated Payment method, this button will initiate the request on your Payment Device.

Add Transaction - Add a transaction for any available/alternate payment methods assigned when assigning a VCT.

Once you add a transaction, it will show under the Transaction section. The entry will show a timestamp of the payment, payment method, amount details, and change due. Once the payment has been applied, the Totals section of the ticket will update for the amount paid.

Note: If you need to remove the transaction, click the Cancel button under the Actions column.

Once the ticket is balanced, click Closed to progress the ticket to Closed status and move any items on the ticket to Sold status. If there is a customer attached to the ticket, you will have the option to save the ticket in other statuses, if you need to return to the ticket at a later time.

Note: Moving tickets to Closed status is a crucial step in adequately recording information for reports. Closing a ticket affects all aspects of your business including accounting reports, collecting tax information, COGS reporting, and inventory management.
