A Category Tree is a structured grouping of categories for your Catalog Items, organized in a parent/child hierarchy. It provides a top-down method for building a visual browsing experience within Item Grids. Together, the Category Tree and Item Grid create an intuitive, drill-down interface for selecting products and services.

These features help structure your offerings in a way that enhances both customer interaction (using the Qsite Opportunities Portal) and staff efficiency when adding products and services to tickets. By implementing a Category Tree, you can create a seamless, user-friendly navigation system that elevates your point-of-sale experience. See a video demo of Item Grids in action here.

Key Benefits

  • Customizable Hierarchy: Organize your catalog intuitively by creating parent categories and subcategories that reflect the unique structure of your business.

  • Visual Browsing Experience: Build an engaging, user-friendly interface that enables customers and staff to navigate your catalog efficiently, reducing search time and improving overall productivity.

  • Functionality: Enable users to refine their search from broad categories down to specific items, ensuring they can quickly find what they need. Modify categories as needed to keep your catalog up to date.

  • Qsite Integration: Seamlessly connect your Category Tree with the Qsite Opportunities portal, allowing customers to explore your catalog effortlessly for a unified shopping experience (Available for Customers on Advanced and Professional plans).

Setting Up Your Category Tree

After establishing your Category Tree, the next step is customizing the browsing experience by configuring the Item Grid. This feature allows users to tailor the visual presentation of categorized items by incorporating images and assigning Catalog Items to respective categories, further enhancing the browsing experience.

View and Customize your Category Tree

Navigate to Company Settings → Categories → Category Trees.

Add New Categories

By default, we have included some basic trees for Items for Sale and Repairable Devices.

To create a new Top-Level Category, click the Add (+) button on the right side of the page and select Top-Level Category.

Note: Click the Edit button next to the category to update the name and other details.

A pop-up will appear, where you can add the new category details. After entering the information, be sure to Save your changes.

  • Name (Required) – Enter the category name.

  • Order (Optional) – Assign a number (0-99) to control the display order in dropdown selections and lists. A lower number places the item higher in the list.

  • Image Select – Drag and drop files or select an image from the Image Library to add graphics to the Item Grid.

Category Customization

Categories are fully customizable, allowing you to be as specific as needed. For example, a Repair Device Tree could be structured as Device Type (Smartphone) → Brand (Apple) → Model Line (iPhone 15) → Device.

Note: If you want to be even more specific, you can create subcategories for attributes like Internal Memory, Color, and more. Since your catalog items will be linked to these categories, ensure your item tree is only as detailed as necessary to match your catalog.

Category Arrangement

You can rearrange categories using a simple click-and-drag motion. When you hover over a category and see the hand icon, click, hold, and drag the category to its desired position.

Add New Sub-Categories

Click the bracket (>) to expand a parent category and view its subcategories. To add more subcategories, click +Add to… at the bottom of the list.
