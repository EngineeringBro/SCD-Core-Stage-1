Invoicing is a feature available to users on our Advanced and Professional plans. You can bill customers for batches of tickets on invoices with a payment period (Net Terms) to indicate when you should be paid for those invoiced goods/services.

This article will walk through the workflow for billing setup and payment for customers who will be using a third-party financing or leasing option as a payment method to pay for any service or retail item through your location. This method is best utilized if you will receive virtual card payments from the financing company. We recommend utilizing billing agents for this method, so you are able to track the sales for that particular financing/leasing program separately.

If you will be utilizing ACH/Check payments provided by the financing/leasing company, see Invoicing: Third-Party Financing Payment Options with ACH or Check.

Note: For this demonstration, we will assume that no sales tax is collected, which is common for certain lease/finance transactions.

Create a Billing Agent for the Finance Company

On the Navigation Bar, go to Customers → Billing Agents.

Add a new Billing Agent and fill out the appropriate information, using the finance company's details.

Create a Ticket and Attach the Billing Agent

1. Create a new Quick Sale or Repair ticket and attach your customer. Under the customer information, make sure Tax Override is checked and the Tax Percentage has been entered as 0%.

Note: A user with the Shift Lead role or higher will have access to this option.

2. Assign the Financing Company as the billing agent.

3. Add items to the ticket that will be paid using the financing option. Any items that will not be paid under financing should be sold out on a separate ticket.

4. Process the application through the financing company and gain approval for the ticket total and obtain all approval information based on your company workflow procedure. Enter all of the approval information into the notes section at the bottom of the ticket.

5. Once all steps have been followed and you have gained approval from the financing company, you may process the payment like a standard credit card payment.

  • If you are using an Integrated Payment solution, click Request Payment and key in the credit card details on your payment device. You can process the payment like a standard card payment.

  • If you are not using an integrated payment method, process and key in the transaction like you would with other credit card transactions. Then click Add Transaction and enter the payment details to be logged on the ticket and click Submit Payment.

6. Once your payment has been processed and the balance is $0, you can close the ticket.
