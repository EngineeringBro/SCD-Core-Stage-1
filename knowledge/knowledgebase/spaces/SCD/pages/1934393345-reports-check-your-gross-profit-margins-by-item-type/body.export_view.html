Viewing the Profitability by Type Report

1. On the Navigation Bar, click Reports.

2. On the Reports page, click on the Sales dropdown along the top row of report categories. From the available list, click Profitability by Type.

3. Use the available filters to scope results based on the assigned parameters.

  • Date Range - Select a date range for your scope of results.

  • Location - Select a specific location or run an aggregate report for multiple stores.

  • Ticket Status - By default, reporting will focus on Closed tickets. You can select to focus on Closed tickets, Open tickets, or All tickets.

    • Note: Open tickets are considered projected totals, per RepairQ Accounting Principles and are not considered as revenue.

  • Warranty Providers - Filter by specific warranty providers, or include all.

  • Include Labor Costs - Labor is added by hours entered on ticket items and the staff member's pay rate found in their Staff User Profile.

4. Once you have set your filters, click Apply Filters to run the report.

Note: You can click Hide/Show Filters on the upper-right side of the page to collapse/expand the filters when viewing reports. You can also click Sales By Catalog Item to view the Sales by Catalog Item Report to view a sales breakdown of catalog items sorted by their Item Type. 

Profitability by Item Type

The Profitability by Item Type report will show a summarized breakdown for each item type with details for the following:

  • Gross Sales - A sum of ticket item subtotals and excludes any restock fees, discounts, returns, and taxes applied.

  • Gross Returns - A sum of returns on 

  • Restock Fees

  • Net Discounts - A total of any discounts applied to the sale minus returns discounts.

  • Net Sales - A total of gross sales with subtracted net discounts and gross returns.

  • COGS

  • Gross Profit - A total of net sales with subtracted COGS amount.

  • GPM% (Gross Profit Margin Percentage) -  The total of (net sales minus COGS) divided by net sales.

 Totals for all columns can be found at the bottom row of the report.
