A Repair Ticket documents the entire repair process for customer-submitted damaged or broken devices. A ticket will include owner information, device condition, triage notes, requested repairs, parts/labor usage, and quality checks. RepairQ uses an assembly-line process to track the status of the device throughout the repair cycle. This helps ensure that the repaired device meets quality standards before being returned to the customer. We also offer various reports to track COGS, revenue, productivity, and more.

Create a New Repair Ticket

On the Navigation Bar, go to Tickets → + Repair. Under the Customer & Billing Agent section, use the search box to search for an existing customer or use the + New Customer button to create a new customer and attach them to the ticket. It is a requirement that a customer is added to the ticket in order to save the ticket progress.

Add a Repair Device

Under the Repair Devices section, use any of the available methods to add a repair device to the ticket. Keep in mind that only items in your catalog that are marked as Repairable will appear when searching for devices. For more information on setting up your catalog for repair devices, see Catalog Items: Creating Sellable, Repairable, and Trade-in Devices.

  • Device History (A) - This section shows previously repaired devices saved to the customer’s profile. If the device in question has been repaired before, select it from the list, update any out-of-date information, and add the required diagnostic notes. When finished, click Apply Initial Diagnostics to add the device to the ticket.

  • Create New (B) - If Catalog Item Quick Create is active, you can add a new Repair Device to your catalog without leaving the ticket. This should only be done if you can’t find a catalog item for the device on the ticket.

  • Look Up (C) - This will bring up the Add Repair Device Pop-up where you can search for devices by category or search your Item Grid for the corresponding device.

  • Smart Search (D) - Search your catalog for repairable devices by keyword, SKU, UPC, and other identifiers.

Add a New Repair Device

In this scenario, we’ve selected a new device via Smart Search. Enter device details and click Add Device.

  • Description - Enter any details to identify the device.

  • Serial Number - Log the serial number/IMEI of the device.

  • Password - If the device has an alphanumeric password, you can log it for reference.

  • Carrier - Enter the carrier details for the device, if needed.

  • Diagnostic Cotes/Complaint - Enter any triage issue for the device, e.g., broken screen, water damage, etc.

(Optional) There are additional categories for Estimate and Logistics that you can fill out to organize your repair process.

  • Not to Exceed - If a customer has a spending limit for a repair/service, you can enter that limit here. If the amount due on the ticket exceeds this amount, you will receive a warning.

  • Promised On - If you commit to the repair being completed by a specific date, you can set the date here. If you do not close the ticket before the agreed-upon date, you will receive a warning. The available dates and times are limited to your store hours listed in Location Settings. This is to ensure you don’t set a date while your store is closed.

  • In Possession - You can record where the phone is physically located e.g., "In possession" in "Bin 6".

  • In Transit - If this device is being shipped out of the store to be repaired, mark "In Transit" and record the Shipper and Tracking Number.

Add Ticket Items (Services, Parts, Accessories, etc.)

After adding a repair device to the ticket, you can include services, labor, parts, and other catalog items to track the repair process and any additional sales. Inventory-tracked parts are pulled from in-stock inventory, while non-inventoried items—such as labor, flat-rate services, or non-inventoried items—can be added without affecting stock levels. Each item appears as a separate line item on the ticket and is recorded as a charge. This helps ensure accurate tracking of parts used, services provided, and any accessories sold.

Bundle Services and Parts on a Ticket

RepairQ offers users the ability to attach (i.e. bundle) items under other items on a ticket. When an item is bundled under another item on a ticket, the price of that item does not contribute towards the ticket total. Instead, the price of the bundled item - the child - becomes a part of the existing parent item. This is designed so you can charge a flat fee for a repair service, and internally track your part cost, inventory, and labor cost separately. For more information on this process, see Catalog Items: Bundle Relationships, Auto-Bundling, and Auto-Adding Items.

Save Ticket / Update Progress

After you have entered the customer and device information, you can save the ticket for later use. At the bottom of the page, there are numerous ticket statuses used to identify which stage of the repair process the device is in. Any time you select a ticket status, the ticket will be updated and saved.

When the ticket is saved, it will generate a Ticket ID and be added to your Ticket Queue. At this point, you can edit the ticket to add any repair services, parts used, and attach any payments to balance and close the ticket.

Note: If you need to search for this ticket, you can use the Global Search Bar, Filter the Ticket Queue, or view the customer’s ticket history to locate the repair ticket to finish the process.

Add a Deposit to a Repair Ticket

Depending on your business operations, you may be required to collect a deposit/down payment from the customer during the repair flow. This may cover part/all of the initial cost associated with the repair and can help offset any diagnostic or administrative costs incurred by the business. See Creating a Quick Sale Ticket for additional information on accepting payment on a ticket.

When you collect money before repairing a phone, it is treated as a customer deposit (i.e. Liability), not as revenue. When the ticket is closed, the sales and COGS are accounted for. Any customer deposits paid in advance are posted from the customer deposit liability account to revenue. See RepairQ Accounting Principles for more information.

Print Receipts, Invoices, and Device Labels

If you have printers set up, you can print information for your customer as well as labels to identify the repair device. The print buttons can be found along the top-right of the ticket.

  • Print Icon - Print a receipt for the customer.

  • Page Icon - Print an invoice (8.5” x 11” page) for the customer.

  • QR Icon - Print a label for the device.

Send Notifications to the Customer

If you have established Ticket Notifications, you can send an SMS or email to the customer. You can also set up automatic notifications to notify the customer when the ticket is placed in a specific status.

  • Bell Icon - Send an SMS notification to the customer. The customer’s profile must include SMS details and allow for notifications.

  • Mail Icon - Send an email notification to the customer. The customer’s profile must include an email address and allow for notifications.

Custom Forms

Custom Forms are documents that are completed on tickets to ensure that the sales/repair process is thorough and complete. There are many customizable fields to assign to a form, based on the purpose. Forms can be found below the Repair Device section of the ticket.
