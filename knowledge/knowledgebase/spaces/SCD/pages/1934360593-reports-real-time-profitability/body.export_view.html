The Profitability Report allows you to look at your total net revenue and total expenses for the day/month/quarter/year. We recommend accounting based on Closed tickets only; once a ticket is closed the date will no longer change, and the sales are reported as revenue. By default, filters are set to closed tickets only, because it is the most reliable method for this business model. For further information about accrual-based reporting, see our article, RepairQ Accounting Principles.

Viewing Real-time Profitability Reporting

1. On the Navigation Bar, go to Reports.

2. On the Reports page, click the Sales dropdown along the top row of report categories. From the available list, click Profitability.

3. Use the available filters to scope results based on the assigned parameters. Once you have set your filters, click Apply Filters to run the report.

  • Date Range - Select a date range for your scope of results.

  • Location - Select a specific location or an aggregate report for multiple/all locations. 

  • Status - Choose to view results based on tickets in Closed status, Open status, or all statuses. By default, the report will run for tickets in Closed status, since ticket information is finalized.

  • Warranty Providers - Filter by specific warranty providers or include all.

  • Include Labor Costs - Labor is added by hours entered on ticket items and for the staff member's pay rate found in their Staff Profile.

  • Include Overhead Costs - Include overhead costs defined in your Location Settings.

Profitability Report

The Profitability Report gives you an overhead view of your Profit Margin. The report gives a glance into Income including Net Sales, Gross Sales, and other income, as well as expenses, including Cost of Goods and Services, and Indirect Labor Costs.
