Linking Supplier SKUs to Catalog Items in RepairQ streamlines inventory management and improves order accuracy. By associating a supplier’s unique SKU with your catalog, you can:

  • Ensure precise communication with suppliers when placing orders.

  • Quickly identify and reorder items using supplier-provided references.

  • Consolidate inventory for items purchased from multiple suppliers.

This feature enhances efficiency, minimizes errors, and helps maintain organized, accurate inventory records. In this article, we’ll guide you through assigning Supplier SKUs to your Catalog Items using various methods.

Assign a Supplier SKU to a Catalog Item

While editing a Catalog Item (on the edit page), click Supplier SKUs at the top of the page to add supplier details. A pop-up window will appear, allowing you to click Add Supplier SKU to enter SKU details and select a supplier from your Supplier List. Don’t forget to click Save Changes to ensure your updates are saved.

Note: If the item is sourced from multiple suppliers, you can create multiple entries to reflect each supply source.

Assign a Supplier SKU to an Item on a Purchase Order

Non-Integrated Suppliers

When creating a new Purchase Order, start by selecting a Supplier for the order, then add items to the Item List. In the Supplier SKU field next to each Catalog Item, enter the relevant SKU details. This information will be saved when the Purchase Order is submitted.

Note: The Supplier SKU field is linked to the Supplier assigned to the Purchase Order. If you switch to a different supplier, you can enter a different Supplier SKU for the item, and it will be saved in the list of assigned Supplier SKUs. Overwriting the Supplier SKU for an existing item will link an additional SKU to that Catalog Item.

Integrated Suppliers

When creating a new Purchase Order, select the Integrated Supplier then add items to the Item List. Click the Magnifying Glass next to the current supplier and search the supplier’s catalog for the correct SKU. Select it to assign the new SKU. Once the order is placed, the SKU will be mapped to the item.

Add an Additional Supplier SKU

There may be situations where you need to change the Supplier SKU linked to a catalog item. Selecting a new Supplier SKU for an existing item will link an additional SKU to that Catalog Item.

Common reasons for updating SKUs include:

  • Upgrading to a Different Product Tier
    For example, you might switch from ordering a mid-tier, medium-grade part to a top-tier, premium-grade alternative from the same supplier.

  • Supplier SKU Changes
    Suppliers may notify you of updated SKUs for the same product. Updating this information ensures your records remain accurate and aligns your inventory with the supplier’s current catalog.

View Assigned Supplier SKUs

To view Supplier SKU details for a Catalog Item, click Supplier SKUs at the top of the item details page.

Edit/Delete Supplier SKUs

When editing a Catalog Item, click Supplier SKUs at the top of the page. You can overwrite an existing SKU or use the Delete Button (Red X) to erase the entry. Be sure to click Save Changes to update your list.
