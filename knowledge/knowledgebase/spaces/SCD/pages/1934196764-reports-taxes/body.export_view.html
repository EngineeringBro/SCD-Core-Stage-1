The Tax Report provides a breakdown of total sales tax by zip code. We recommend basing your accounting on closed tickets only, as the date of a closed ticket remains fixed, and the sales are recognized as revenue. By default, filters are set to display closed tickets only, which is the most reliable method for this business model. For information about accrual-based reporting, see our article, RepairQ Accounting Principles.

Viewing the Tax Report

On the Navigation Bar, go to Reports. On the Reports page, click the Accounting dropdown along the top row of report categories. From the available list, click Tax Report.

Use the available filters to scope results based on the assigned parameters. Once you have set your filters, click Apply Filters to run the report.

(Optional) You can click To Excel on the upper-right of the report to export data in an Excel-friendly format. You can click Hide/Show filters to collapse/expand the filters.

  • Date Range - Select a date range for your scope of results.

  • Location - Select a specific location or an aggregate report for multiple/all locations. 

  • Status - Choose to view results based on tickets in Closed status, Open status, or all statuses. By default, the report will run for tickets in Closed status, since ticket information is finalized.

  • Billing Agent - Filter by specific billing agents or include all.

  • Item Type - Filter by specific Catalog Item Types.

Tax Report

The Tax Report delivers a summarized breakdown of taxes collected on all revenue, categorized into taxable and non-taxable amounts. This includes taxes related to returned taxable and non-taxable revenue, as well as net taxes.

The report organizes tax information based on the zip code of your business address, as configured in Location Settings. If a tax override was applied to a ticket, the report will also include an additional line item reflecting the customer’s zip code from their Customer Profile. In cases where no address is assigned to the Customer Profile, the tax data will default to being recorded under the business address's zip code.

Taxes per Ticket

It's important to note that there isn't a specific report dedicated to a breakdown of taxes recorded on each ticket, however, users can still access this data by filtering the Ticket Queue for tickets updated to "Closed" status within the same date range of the Tax Report. Users can view the taxes recorded on each ticket by updating the Column View to include the "Tax" field. You can also export the list in an Excel-friendly format. The total tax displayed here will match the "Tax Collected" amount in the Tax Report.
