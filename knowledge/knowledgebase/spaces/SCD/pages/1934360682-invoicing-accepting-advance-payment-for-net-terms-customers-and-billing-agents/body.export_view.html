Users have the ability to accept advance payment for net terms customers and Billing Agents and record the payment as a credit to their account profile to be used as payment when invoicing the customer. If an advance payment is received, it should be credited to the account profile based on who will be responsible for payment. A customer account will be used to pay for repairs recorded on tickets under that account only. A Billing Agent account will be used to pay for repairs recorded on tickets under any customer ticket with that Billing Agent assigned. This article will demonstrate instructions for recording advance payment and applying the payment to invoices.

Note: Accepting advance payment for a Billing Agent or net terms customer is not a transaction, so it is not recorded on a VCT. The payment is accepted outside of RepairQ but the credit will be applied in-app under the account profile.

Record Advance Payment from Customer or Billing Agent

1. Look up the Net Terms customer or Billing Agent using the Global Search Bar.

2. When viewing the account profile, click the Credit tab on the left column of categories.

3. This page will list the current deposit balance, credit information, as well as a complete list of credit and debit transactions. Click the Add button (+) to apply a credit.

4. A Balance Adjustment pop-up will appear. Fill out the adjustment details. Make sure the amount is equal to the advance payment and note the reason for the adjustment. When you are satisfied with your changes, click Save.

Record a Ticket Payment from Customer Deposit Account

If a customer has a credit balance on their account, any amount up to this credit balance can be applied as payment on a ticket. To record payment from a customer deposit account on a ticket, use the Store Credit payment method when adding a transaction to the ticket.

Note: The Accept Store Credit option must be selected prior to opening your VCT.

Record an Invoice Payment from a Customer or Billing Agent Deposit Account

If a net terms customer or Billing Agent has a credit balance on their account, any amount up to this credit balance can be applied as payment on an Invoice. You can record a payment from the Remittance page for the invoice. Under the Apply Payment section, enter the payment amount and select Store Credit as the payment method.

Reconcile Managed Services at the End of the Billing Term

At the end of the term, any remaining balance in the customer/Billing Agent account can be reconciled using a Repair Ticket or Quick Sale Ticket. For optimal tracking, a new Catalog Item can be set up to track Managed Services Reconciliation fees. Since the remaining balance will vary for each contract and bill period, it is important that you will be able to enter the exact amount at the time the reconciliation ticket is recorded. When creating your Catalog Item, make sure the option Can Override Price is selected.
