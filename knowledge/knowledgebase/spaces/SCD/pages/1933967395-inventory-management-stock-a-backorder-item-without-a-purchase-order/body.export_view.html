When a user adds an inventory item to a ticket that has zero quantity in stock it will show as a backorder item. RepairQ will create a placeholder entry in your inventory for your records. A ticket with a backorder item cannot be closed until you have replaced the placeholder with an in-stock item. Backorder items can be fulfilled manually from the ticket or automatically through a Purchase Order (PO) replenishment. If the same item has been back ordered on multiple tickets, a PO will fulfill older tickets first. The reconciled PO will also show a list of backorder fulfillments.

The tutorial below will walk you through the process of stocking a backorder item on a ticket without utilizing a PO. It is understood that the item has been ordered and received, and the user is updating the inventory item to complete the ticket.

Stock a Backorder Item by Editing Inventory Details on a Ticket

1. Search for the ticket with the backorder item. Click the details link next to the Catalog Item to open the details in a pop-up window. 

Note: When viewing the ticket details, you can verify that the item is in Back Ordered status under the Ticket Items section. 

2. On the Catalog Item Details pop-up, click the view item in inventory button to open the inventory details in a new tab.

3. On the Inventory Item Management page, you can see details and the Change Log for the item. On the upper-right side of the page, click Edit Item.

4. On the Inventory Update page, select a new Inventory Status for the item. Once you are satisfied with your selection, Save your changes at the bottom of the page.

  • Pulled - Select this status if you have the item in your possession and are ready to reserve it for the ticket.

  • Sold - Select this status if you have already used/sold this item and the item is no longer in your possession.

5. When viewing the ticket, you can see that the item has now been pulled to the appropriate status. You can edit the ticket to take further action to progress and/or close the ticket.
