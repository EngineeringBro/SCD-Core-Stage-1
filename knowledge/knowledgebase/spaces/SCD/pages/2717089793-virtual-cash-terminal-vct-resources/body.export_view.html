Virtual Cash Terminals (VCTs) track cash flow and card payments on tickets, helping ensure accurate register balancing at the end of the day. Our optional integrated Payment systems give users a secure solution for accepting payments on a payment terminal and seamlessly posting those transactions to a RepairQ ticket. This streamlines the checkout process and reduces accounting errors compared to manually recording payments.

VCTs are now optional to accommodate varied business needs, allowing transactions to be recorded outside our software. Two new settings enable users to customize the ticket closure process without requiring a VCT or payment, ensuring that inventory is still managed effectively within RepairQ.

If you open a VCT, it must be closed daily for cash reconciliation. This process, handled by Shift Leads or Managers, should be performed on each computer or device paired to a VCT. Closing your VCT daily helps reconcile cash counts and audit for payment discrepancies.

Note: VCTs and cash reconciliation do not contribute to sales or COGS reporting, as this information is pulled directly from ticket data. They should not be used to calculate sales projections.
