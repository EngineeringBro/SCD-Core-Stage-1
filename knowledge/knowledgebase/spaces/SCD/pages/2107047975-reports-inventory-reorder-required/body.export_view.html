The Inventory Reorder Required is a quick reference list of items that have fallen below their assigned Reorder Point (ROP). This can be used to track and manage inventory levels, indicating when it’s time to restock specific items to ensure availability. The included Reorder Quantity (ROQ) field provides a quantity for your order that will meet the ROP after fulfilling any backorders. This report can also be exported and used as a Purchase Order template for quckly creating Purchase Orders in RepairQ.

Viewing Inventory Reorder Required Reporting

1. On the Navigation Bar, go to Reports.

2. On the Reports page, click on the Inventory dropdown along the top row of report categories. From the available list, click Inventory Reorder Required.

3. Use the available filters to scope results based on the assigned parameters. Once you have set your filters, click Apply Filters to run the report.

  • Location - Select to view results from a specific location or an aggregate report of multiple locations.

  • Manufacturer - Select to view a list of items from a specific manufacturer or an aggregate report of all manufacturers.

  • Supplier - This optional field is used when generating a Purchase Order export. The selection will automatically populate in the Supplier field of the import document.

4. (Optional) Click To Excel on the upper-right side of the report page to export an Excel-friendly document for your records. You can also click Hide/Show Filters on the upper-right side of the page to collapse/expand the filters when viewing reports.

Inventory Reorder Required

This report shows all items that meet your reorder criteria. The list gives the location, inventory item details, and the current stock details for items.

  • Instock - Your current In-stock quantity of the inventory item. This does not include any items in Pulled status.

  • Ordered - Your current Ordered quantity of the inventory item.

  • ROP (Reorder Point) - This column shows the default quantity (set by the user) used to notify users when the in-stock quantity for an item falls below the assigned amount.

  • ROA (Reorder Amount) - This column shows the default quantity (set by the user) to reorder when adding an item to a Purchase Order.

  • ROQ (Reorder Quantity) - This column shows the actual amount requiring reorder, which takes into consideration any ordered and/or backorder items. This ensures that your order will meet the ROP after fulfilling any backorders. This total is also used as the Reorder Quanity when generating a Purchase Order import sheet.

    • For instance, an item (the Generic iPhone Case in the above screenshot) with a Reorder Point of 10 with no In-stock items, no Ordered items, and one Backordered item will show a Reorder Quantity of 11. This is to ensure that after you fulfill the backorder item, you do not fall below your Reorder Point.

    • Using the equation (ROP - (ROA+Ordered - Backordered)): (10 - (0 + 0 - 1 ) = 11.

Generate POs Export

The inventory Reorder Required report can also be used to auto-populate a Purchase Order Import Template. First, select a Supplier from the filter, which will preload the Supplier field in the template. Next, click Generate POs at the bottom of the report to export the Purchase Order Template.

The export will automatically populate any assigned Supplier SKUs, and the Qty and Supplier fields reflect the Supplier and ROQ fields from the report. The Qty field is adjusted to ensure that you meet your assigned ROA after fulfilling any backorders.

For more information on this process, see Purchase Orders: Importing and Uploading.
