Quick Sale Tickets are used to process any sales of inventory or services. The diagram below outlines the general flow of the ticket as well as additional explanations for terms, behaviors, and actions available during this process. Ticket statuses also work with our Automated Notification feature, saving you time by automatically sending communications to customers when placing a ticket in a specific status.

Quick Sale Ticket Status Flow

Create a New Ticket

When creating a new Quick Sale ticket, you can choose whether or not to associate a customer with the ticket. If you link a customer with the ticket, you will be able to save the ticket by progressing it to a status other than Closed. This allows you to save the ticket for later if you need to pick up where you left off. You can also use different statuses to flag what part of the sale process the ticket is in.

Ticket history is also recorded under the customer’s profile; should they have any questions in the future, you have an easy reference to pull any tickets/receipts under their name. Users can search the Ticket Queue based on customer information, ticket status, and other fields.

If you choose not to link a customer with the ticket (anonymous), you won’t be able to save the ticket to any status other than Closed. Once the ticket has been saved, you can search the Ticket Queue by the original ticket number, however, customer search is not applicable.

The exception to this is if you accept payment with one of our Integrated Payment solutions. The auto-save feature during the transaction process will save the ticket and generate a Ticket ID, regardless of whether or not a customer is lined to the ticket.

Saved Ticket Status/Transition Status

After you have attached a customer to a ticket, you can progress/save it to one the following statuses: New, Hold/Backordered, Layaway, Waiting for Payment, and Closed. Saving a ticket will generate a Ticket ID and any inventoried items on the ticket will be pulled from stock at this time. Each time you change the status of a ticket, the details will be updated and saved. Tickets can move interchangebly between statuses to reflect which stage in the sale process it is currently in, with exception to New status. Once a ticket is saved, it is also searchable in your Ticket Queue.

  • New - This identifies the ticket as newly created and can be recalled later from the Ticket Queue or Global Search for further action. Once a ticket has been moved away from New, it cannot be put back into this status.

    • You can move a ticket in New status to Waiting for Payment, Hold/Backordered, or Layaway.

  • Hold/Backordered - This identifies the ticket as on hold and pending further action. If an out-of-stock item/part has been added to the ticket (backorder item), the ticket cannot be Closed until the backorder item has been replenished or removed from the ticket.

    • You can move a ticket in Hold/Backordered status to Ready for Pickup, Waiting for Payment, or Layaway.

  • Waiting for Payment - This identifies the ticket as ready for payment from the customer.

    • You can move a ticket in Waiting for Payment status to Hold/Backordered or Layaway.

  • Layaway - This identifies the ticket as secured for purchase with a deposit. At your discretion, a customer can pay towards the amount due until the ticket is balanced and can be closed.

    • You can move a ticket in Layaway status to Ready for Pickup or Layaway.

  • Ready for Pickup - This identifies the ticket as ready for the customer to pick up the merchandise. This status is only available from Hold/Backordered or Layway status. The item on this ticket may have been fulfilled from a backorder or has been on layaway and is now paid off.

    • You can move a ticket in Ready for Pickup status to Layaway, Hold/Backordered, or Waiting for Payment.

  • Closed - This identies the ticket as finalized and complete. Any inventoried ticket items have been moved to Sold status, and sales details (COGS, labor, etc.) have been recorded for reporting.

Ticket Reconciliation

When you are ready to reconcile the ticket, the balance due from the customer must be zero ($0.00) before you can move the ticket into Closed status. RepairQ uses an accural-based system, so payment before closing the ticket is considered as a deposit (liability) and not revenue. Closed tickets record any revenue, COGS , taxes, etc. for accounting.

There are a few ways you can balance the ticket:

  • Take payment from the customer - If you have a Virtual Cash Terminal (VCT) open and assigned, you can accept payment or issue a return for the customer. Once there is no balance due on the ticket, it can be closed.

  • Remove items from the ticket - When a ticket item is removed from the ticket, the amount due for that item is also removed from the balance due. If you do not intend on selling out this product/service, you should remove it from the ticket.

  • Discount items- At your discretion, you can apply a discount towards the price of the item to reconcile the ticket. Discounts applied are tracked in reporting, and admins have the ability to grant discount allowances for each staff member.

  • Waive fees - A manager can waive fees for goods/services on the ticket. Waived charges are tracked in reporting.

  • Mark an item as defective/damaged - Upon inspection of your goods, you may find that items are not in a sellable condition. Users can mark an item as defective and follow your supplier’s Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA) process or as damaged, which will adjust the item out of the system as a loss (shrinkage). This information can be tracked in reporting.

Closed Ticket

Once you close a ticket, the transaction is considered complete. RepairQ uses an accural-based system, so payment before closing the ticket is considered as a deposit (liability) and not revenue. Closed tickets record any revenue, COGS , taxes, etc. for accounting.

Closed tickets may be reopened for returns, exchanges, return repairs, etc. The balance for the ticket must be balanced to zero before it can be saved again. Closed tickets can be searched a few ways. Examples include: by Ticket ID, by filtering for Closed status in your Ticket Queue, and by customer (if they were linked to the ticket before closing it).
