RepairQ now offers flexible currency settings, allowing users to customize bill and coin denominations used for cash transactions and nightly reconciliation. By default, the currency is set to US-based values, and users can replace these with international denominations that suit their local needs. For example, a location might operate with coins like $0.05, $0.10, $0.20, etc.

This customization ensures that the experience aligns with the currency in use at your location, making cash handling more efficient and accurate. The configured currency values are reflected in the Cash Reconciliation during cash terminal closures, ensuring consistency between what’s been recorded in the system and the actual cash on hand. Benefits include:

  • Increased accessibility - Global users can now set currency denominations that match their local cash handling practices, eliminating confusion with irrelevant coin or bill values.

  • Streamlined cash management - The system automatically integrates your custom denominations into terminal closures and reports, simplifying the end-of-day cash count process.

  • Improved accuracy - Customized values help ensure that all cash transaction records and reconciliations match the currency used, reducing errors.

Assign Cash Terminal Currency

Note: Please ensure all Virtual Cash Terminals (VCTs) are closed and reconciled before reassigning currency settings, as changes will take effect immediately.

Log into RepairQ as an Admin. On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings. On the Settings Overview page, under the Account section, click General Settings.

On the General Settings page, in the Currency section, enter the denomination amount and click +Add. The amount will be added to the list, and denominations on the Reconciliation Report appear in the same order in which they were added. Be sure to Save your settings after adding the list of currencies.

Note: Use a decimal for coin amounts under a dollar (e.g., 0.25) and a whole number for full dollar amounts (e.g., 1). Denominations are entered on the Reconciliation Report in the same order as when added to the list.

Updated Currency on the Cash Reconciliation Report

The Cash Reconciliation report will reflect the denominations you've set, ensuring that all totals are calculated based on your custom currency settings.
