Admins can now update Purchase Order settings to allow users with the Inventory Manager role (or above) to reopen reconciled purchase orders. This is helpful for POs closed in error before receiving all items. While this may seem like a departure from the default behavior, it puts more control in the hands of our users, allowing for greater flexibility in managing inventory flow and operations. This empowerment can improve efficiency and customer service outcomes without contacting the Support team.

Note: Reopening closed POs will change the dates in your records. We strongly recommend you align this setting with your inventory process.

Allowing for Purchase Orders to be Reopened

This allowance is at the global level, which affects all locations on your database.

On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings. On the Settings page, under the Catalog section, click Purchase Orders.

On the Purchase Order Settings page, under the General Settings section, you will see an option for “Allow Reopening Reconciled Orders.” Select the timeframe for opening reconciled POs and Save your settings.

  • When closed on the same day - The user can reopen a PO the same day it was reconciled.

  • When closed in the same week - The user can reopen a PO within 7 days of reconciling.

  • When closed in the same month - The user can reopen a PO within 30 days of reconciling.

  • When closed in the same year - The user can reopen a PO within 365 days of reconciling.

  • Never allow reopening closed tickets - This will restrict the ability to reopen a PO after it has been reconciled. If you need to make changes, create a new PO and receive the items into inventory.

Reopening a Reconciled Purchase Order

In this scenario, the PO was partially received and reconciled before receiving the remainder of the inventory.

When viewing the Purchase Order, click the Properties button on the upper right of the page.

Use the Purchase Order Properties modal to update the Status field, add any notes, and then Save your changes.

The PO will be moved out of Reconciled status and revert to the selected status. You can now edit the PO to receive additional items as needed. After reconciling again, the reconciled date will be updated to reflect the new reconciliation date in the purchase order change Log.
