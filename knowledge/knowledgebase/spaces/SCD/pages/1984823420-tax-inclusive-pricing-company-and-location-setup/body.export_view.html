Depending on your local country/state requirements, you may be required to display tax-inclusive pricing for customers. RepairQ includes customization features to display tax-inclusive pricing (combined price and tax) of items and services when navigating lists, creating tickets, and viewing receipts/invoices. Depending on your local country/state requirements, you may also be required to display tax-inclusive pricing for customers. This allows you to offer a consistent, no-surprise pricing method, so customers know exactly what they will pay in full during checkout.

Once this setting is enabled, RepairQ will display the combined retail price + any taxes assigned to your item types.

Displaying Tax-Inclusive Pricing for Your Company

Log into RepairQ as an Admin. From the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings. On the Settings Overview page, click General Settings under the Account section.

On the General Settings page, turn on the Tax-inclusive Pricing setting and Save your changes.

Define Tax ID for Your Location

Log into RepairQ as an Admin. From the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Location Settings. On the Settings Overview page, click Location Info under the Account section.

Tax Settings are under your store contact fields. Update these settings and Save your changes.

  • Tax ID - Enter your assigned tax ID number. If you are required by local/state tax laws to display your Tax ID, it can be displayed on receipts.

  • Tax Inclusive Pricing - (Location-Level setting) When this is selected, RepairQ will display tax-inclusive pricing on Tickets, Catalog Items, Inventory Items, and Lists throughout the software.

Tax-Inclusive Pricing UI Displays in RepairQ

Once you have saved your settings, you will see changes to the following fields, labels, and lists.

Note: The “Tax Inclusive Pricing” display layout option is available in Ticket General Settings and Invoice Template setup. This allows you to configure behaviors and appearance for tickets to align with GST standards. See the Next Steps section for more details.

Tax-inclusive Catalog Items

When creating a catalog item, the Price fields will update for Price, incl. tax. When entering information into these fields, enter the tax-inclusive amount.

Tax-inclusive Catalog Item List

When viewing the Catalog List (and other related lists), pricing column names will display the Price, including tax.

Tax-inclusive Price Inventory Item

When viewing the details for an inventory item, the pricing column names will display the Price, including tax.

Tax-inclusive Price Overrides and Discounts

When adding an item to a ticket and a user applies a discount or a price override (can override price must be active for the Catalog Item), the Unit Price Editor will appear. The user can choose whether the unit price will be adjusted to a pre or post-tax (tax-inclusive) amount. By default, the price will be adjusted for the tax-inclusive amount.
