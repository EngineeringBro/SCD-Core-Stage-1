Sometimes, you may need to remove a ticket to fix an error, undo an accidental entry, or keep your records accurate without affecting sales and inventory data. Depending on the situation, you can either delete or void a ticket.

Deleting Tickets

Tickets can only be deleted if they are not in Closed status and do not contain any transaction information. If the ticket is eligible for deletion, remove all items, save the ticket, and delete it from the Ticket List.

Voiding Tickets

Voiding a closed ticket is often more efficient than processing a return because it automatically reverses payments and restocks any returned items. However, tickets can only be voided on the same business day they are closed. After that, tickets are locked and can no longer be voided. Tickets in Void status do not affect profitability reporting but remain in the system for historical records.

If you plan to void a ticket that has not been closed, we recommend removing all items, refunding any transactions, and closing the ticket. Once closed, you’ll have until the end of the business day to void it.

Note: To void a ticket, you must have an assigned Virtual Cash Terminal (VCT) with the correct payment methods configured.

Delete a Ticket

If a ticket has no items or transactions, you can delete it from your Ticket List. First, locate the ticket, click the Options button under the Actions column, and select Delete from the available actions. You will be asked to confirm deletion, and once deleted, you will receive confirmation that the ticket is completely removed from the system, with no records retained.

Void a Closed Ticket

To void a ticket, you must have an assigned Virtual Cash Terminal (VCT) with the correct payment methods configured.

First locate the ticket you need to void. If needed, use the filters on the Ticket List to find tickets in Closed status. On the Ticket View page, click the Void button in the ticket actions row.

Clicking Void will reopen the ticket for review. The Void Ticket Items section will display any items that will be refunded and returned to inventory (if applicable). The Transactions section will generate a pending payout based on the original payment method.

To complete the process, click Void at the bottom-right of the screen. You will be redirected to the Ticket View page, where you can print a receipt, add notes, and confirm that the ticket is now in Void status.
