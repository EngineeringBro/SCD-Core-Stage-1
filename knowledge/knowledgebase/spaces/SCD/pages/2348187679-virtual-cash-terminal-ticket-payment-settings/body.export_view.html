At the core of RepairQ lies the crucial process of balancing and closing tickets, ensuring accurate accounting and inventory management. Usually, balancing a ticket involves various methods, including taking payments from an open Virtual Cash Terminal (VCT), applying discounts, and waiving fees. We understand that business needs may vary and transactions may be recorded outside our software. To accommodate these needs, we’ve introduced two new settings to allow users to customize their ticket closure process without requiring a VCT or payment, ensuring that inventory is managed effectively within RepairQ.

Aligning with our Accounting Methods, when a ticket is closed the sales and COGS are accounted for, so (depending on your settings) there is no longer a requirement to post payment on a ticket, as long as it is moved to Closed status.

  • Record Payments via VCT - Record payments and cash flow, ensuring accurate cash flow reconciliation by providing a reliable method for tracking cash and card transactions. Tickets with a balance due must have a payment recorded via the VCT to balance to zero before they can be closed. This process is vital for balancing your register nightly and maintaining precise financial records.

  • Cashless Payment Recording - If you track cash flow through other software or primarily accept payments from virtual options like PayPal, Apple Pay, Venmo, Square, etc. you can record payments without opening a cash terminal, simplifying the transaction process. Tickets with a balance due must have a payment recorded to balance to zero before they can be closed.

  • Ticket Closure Without Payment - Users who rely on other accounting software for transactions can now close tickets even when a balance is due, eliminating the need to record a duplicate payment in RepairQ. No payment is required before closing the ticket.

Note: If VCT or Ticket Payment requirements are turned off, there is no need for the Cash Reconciliation report. If both settings are turned on, transactions can be recorded regardless of a Cash Terminal assignment. However, transactions recorded without a terminal assignment will not appear on the Reconciliation Report. If both settings are enabled, we advise establishing your procedures for transaction recording to ensure accuracy.

Require a VCT Assignment Before Accepting Payment

This method will require users to assign a Cash Terminal before they can accept payments on tickets. This ensures accurate cash flow and end-of-day reconciliation reporting.

On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings. On the Settings Overview page, under the Account section, click General Settings.

On the Settings page, update the following fields, then Save your changes.

  • Cash Terminals Enabled - ON

  • Ticket Payments Enabled - OFF

Apply a Payment to a Ticket Without Requiring a VCT Assignment (Cash Terminals Disabled)

Your business may utilize alternative accounting software for tracking payments, or you may only take virtual payments. In that case, you can bypass the requirement to open a VCT before recording payments on a ticket. Tickets must still have a zero balance before they can be closed.

Note: If both settings are turned on, transactions can be recorded regardless of a Cash Terminal assignment. However, please note that transactions made without a terminal assignment will not appear on the Reconciliation Report. If both settings are enabled, we advise establishing your own procedures for transaction recording to ensure accuracy.

On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings → Account General Settings. On the Settings page, update the following fields, then Save your changes. The options to assign a VCT and manage Payment Devices will be removed from the UI, and you can freely add transactions to tickets as needed.

  • Cash Terminals Enabled - OFF

  • Ticket Payments Enabled - ON

Close a Ticket with a Balance Due (VCTs and Ticket Payments Disabled)

Many businesses use our software alongside their existing accounting systems, utilizing our platform primarily for sales and repair ticket management. Admins can update RepairQ settings that disable the requirement of opening a VCT and for payment recording, allowing users to close the ticket (regardless of the balance due) to update inventory levels and finalize the ticket.

On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings → Account General Settings. On the Settings page, update the following fields, then Save your changes.

  • Cash Terminals Enabled - OFF

  • Ticket Payments Enabled - OFF

The options to assign a VCT and manage Payment Devices will be removed from the UI, the Transactions section of tickets will be removed, and you can freely close tickets with a balance due.
