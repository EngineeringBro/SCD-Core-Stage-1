RepairQ Admins can assign roles to staff members to control their access and actions based on their responsibilities. Roles determine what a staff member can do and which locations to access, ensuring each user has the appropriate permissions for their tasks. When creating a new Staff Profile, at least one role must be assigned for one or more locations to enable the user to log in.

For users who require full access, an Admin can assign other admin privileges, granting them all permissions across every location in the database. This flexibility allows you to maintain security, streamline operations, and ensure staff access aligns with your business needs.

  • Customized Permissions - Assign role-specific permissions to control actions like inventory management, ticket creation, and financial reporting.

  • Location-Specific Access - Limit staff access to one or multiple locations, making it easy to manage multi-store operations.

  • Admin Privileges - Restrict sensitive actions to designated administrative roles, reducing the risk of errors or unauthorized changes.

Default Access

Every staff member with an assigned role (or Admin access) for a location will have access to the following capabilities:

  • Basic Operations:

    • Clock in/out

    • Perform price checks

    • Update their User Profile photo

  • Customer Management:

    • View, create, and update customer profiles

    • Add and update customer notes

    • Manage customer devices

  • Inventory Management:

    • View inventory summary

    • Perform inventory counts

    • Add inventory to tickets

  • Ticket Management:

    • View and search the Ticket Queue

    • Create and update ticket information

    • Add and update ticket notes

    • Add/remove ticket items

    • Process ticket sales, returns, or repair returns

    • Print, email, or SMS receipts

Location-Based Roles

The following roles can be assigned to your staff individually. Each role grants specific capabilities, and you can assign one or more roles to a staff member for each location as needed.

Location Admin

The Location Admin role oversees all store operations, including staff management, store settings, and store-level decision-making. Users with this role are granted full permissions for all store-level actions, making it an ideal choice for store owners or managers.

  • Integrations - Set up integrations for payment processing, accounting, and more.

  • Customers - Manage customer credit accounts

  • Catalog - Manage catalog items/manage location overrides

  • Inventory - Add/edit/delete inventory

  • Reports - View all reports

  • Settings - Manage settings

  • Staff - Manage staff

  • Timesheet - View/edit time sheets

  • Waive charges for a ticket item

Manager

The Manager role is responsible for overseeing daily operations and making store-level decisions. Managers can manage staff by adding new employees, assigning locations, viewing timecards, and creating staff announcements. They also have full access to tickets and inventory, limited access to settings, and restricted access to the catalog.

  • Cash Reconciliation

  • Customers - Manage customer credit accounts

  • Inventory - Add/edit/delete items

  • Inventory Counts/transfers/purchase orders

  • Invoicing - Create invoices

  • RMAs - Access to RMA process

  • Reporting - View all reports

  • Staff - Announcements/add staff/assign location/view timesheets

  • Tickets - Create/edit/discount/waive charges/void

  • Tickets - Transfer a ticket to another location

Inventory Manager

The Inventory Manager role includes all the access granted to the Sales or Technician roles, with additional responsibilities specifically for managing inventory. This role allows users to oversee stock levels, perform inventory counts, and manage stock transfers between locations. Inventory Managers can also adjust inventory quantities, process returns, and review inventory reports to ensure accurate stock tracking and efficient operations.

  • Catalog - Manage catalog items

  • Catalog - Manage location overrides

  • Inventory - Add new inventory

  • Inventory - Edit existing inventory

  • Inventory - Delete inventory

  • Inventory Counts - Assign/reconcile Inventory Counts

  • Purchase Order - Create/edit Purchase Orders and Inventory Transfers

  • RMA Tracking - Create/reconcile RMA Batches

Shift Lead

The Shift Lead role is typically assigned to a Sales Representative or Technician who also takes on additional responsibilities to support management. Shift Leads can oversee other employees during their shifts and have elevated permissions, such as voiding tickets or applying discounts beyond the standard staff discount limit.

In addition to these tasks, Shift Leads play a crucial role in cash management. They can open a Virtual Cash Terminal (VCT) at the start of the day, close it at the end of business, and use the Cash Drawer Reconciliation Report to ensure the cash drawer balances correctly. This role is ideal for employees who act as key-holders or are entrusted with managing store operations during their shifts.

  • Tickets - Void/discount tickets

  • Ticket Item - Waive charges

  • Timesheets - View staff timesheets

  • VCT - Open and close VCTs

Sales Representative

The Sales Representative serves as the first point of contact for customers in a retail environment. They assist customers by offering products and services, answering questions, and ensuring a positive in-store experience.

Sales Representatives are responsible for creating Quick Sales or Repair Tickets to process transactions, sell inventory, or document issues with a customer’s device brought in for repair. They can add products and services to tickets, provide pricing details, and collect payments. This role is essential for maintaining smooth front-line operations and delivering excellent customer service.

  • Ticket - Apply discount up to discount limit set in staff settings

  • Ticket - Process pay-in/pay-out transactions

  • Inventory - Purchase used inventory from customers via Tradein ticket

Technician

The Technician primarily works behind the scenes, focusing on repairing customer devices or refurbishing devices purchased from customers to prepare them for resale as used inventory. While they may not interact directly with customers, their role is critical to ensuring high-quality repairs and efficient operations.

Technicians can add parts to tickets, correct errors by pulling or returning parts, and record the labor hours spent on repairs for productivity tracking and reporting. They can also document their work by adding detailed notes to tickets, providing valuable context for both customers and other team members.

Additionally, Technicians may assist with periodic inventory counts and reconciliation, ensuring accurate stock levels for repair parts and tools. This role is ideal for skilled team members dedicated to maintaining device quality and operational efficiency.

  • Tickets – record actual labor hours for productivity tracking & reporting

  • Inventory – add notes/update inventory condition/update inventory status (complete refurb ticket)

Invoicing

The Invoicing role is available on Advanced and Professional plans This role enables users to generate invoices for customers or Billing Agents, process payments on those invoices, and access key financial reports, such as Receivables and Invoice Aging.

  • Generate Invoices and Process Payments - Create invoices for customers or billing agents. Apply payments to invoices, including cash, check, credit card, or other methods.

  • View and Manage Receivables - Access receivables reports to track outstanding invoices and payments.

Location Advisor

The Location Advisor role is typically reserved for area or regional managers who require read-only access to store information. This role allows users to view key store data without the ability to make changes or create new records. A Location Advisor is assigned only to the locations they need to access, ensuring they can monitor operations without modifying sensitive information.

In addition to location-specific access, the Location Advisor has global access to the catalog, enabling them to update and add new items to stores as needed. This role is ideal for overseeing store performance and ensuring consistency across locations while maintaining control over sensitive store data.

Read-only access applies to:

  • Ticket Queue

  • Invoices

  • Customer Lists

  • All Reports

  • Cash Reconciliation

  • Catalog Item Location Overrides

Admin Access

In addition to assigning roles, Admin Access permissions allow overrides of the current staff roles for more granular control. Using this feature, Admins can assign standard roles to users while granting temporary special access permissions to override their default role.

  • System Admin Access - This is the highest level of Admin access. The user will receive override access to all areas of RepairQ for all locations and Analytic Report access. Assign this authority with caution, as the user will have access to all areas of the software.

  • Admin Access - The user will receive Manager-level access and Location Settings access for all locations, excluding settings for Ticket Approval, Ticket Action, and Ticket Resolution Types.

  • Accountant Access - The user will have view-only access to tickets, access to reports for accounting purposes, and the ability to export reports for all locations.

  • No Special Access - The user will receive no additional permissions outside their assigned roles.
