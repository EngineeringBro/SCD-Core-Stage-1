Serial numbers are unique, identification numbers/alphanumeric characters assigned to products to distinguish them individually, rather than an entire range of the same product. In RepairQ, when a catalog item is marked as serialized, a serial number is required before the item can be counted and tracked in the system. If you have items that do not have serial numbers, but would like to assign one to them for tracking purposes, the auto-serialization feature can generate a serial number for those items when added/imported into inventory. This feature also works alongside our Part Core Recovery process to assign a serial number to part cores that are going back to the manufacturer.

Auto-serialization is assigned at the Item Type classification of the Catalog item, so any items that are also marked as serialized will have unique serial numbers generated for them. Catalog items under this Item Type that are inventoried but not serialized will not be affected.

Activate Auto-Serialization for an Item Type

1. On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings.

2. On the Settings Overview page, click Item Types under the Catalog section.

3. On the Item Types Overview page, you can choose to Add Item Type or Edit an existing type. Auto-serialization is defined for each Item Type in your catalog.

Note: You may also notice there is a new Auto Gen Serial column on the list, which notes whether Auto-Seriailze is turned ON or OFF for an Item Type.

4. When creating/changing item type fields, look for the Auto Generate Serial Numbers section. Update these fields, based on your preference. After you have made your changes, be sure to Save your settings.

  • Enabled - This checkbox will enable/disable the Auto Generate feature.

  • Serial Prefix - (Optional) Add a unique prefix that will be part of the generated serial numbers.

    • You can add a prefix to note which serial numbers were generated and not issued by the manufacturer.

  • Serial Format - Select the serial format when generating new serial numbers.

    • With Hyphen - Generates a serial number with a hyphen.

    • Without Hyphen - Generates a serial number without a hyphen.

    • UID Without Hyphen - Generates a unique identifier (UID) that is also the Inventory ID for the item in RepairQ.

      • As your inventory grows over time, the Inventory IDs for each item will increase in numerical digits, meaning if you use this method for serialization, the serial number characters will not be uniform across items.
