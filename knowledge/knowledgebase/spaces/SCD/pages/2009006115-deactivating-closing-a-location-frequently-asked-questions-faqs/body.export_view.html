As your business evolves, you might need to close a location and adjust your subscription in RepairQ. To ensure a smooth transition and maintain seamless operations, follow these important steps before and after deactivating a location:

  • Final Inventory Count - Conduct a final inventory check for the store and transfer any remaining stock to another location.

  • Staff Deactivation - Remove access for any staff members who are no longer with the company from RepairQ.

  • Export Financial Records - Save essential reports, lists, and snapshots for your financial records.

  • Deactivate The Location - Proceed with deactivating the location within RepairQ.

  • Update Your Subscription - Adjust your subscription to reflect the changes in Users/Locations.

Below, you’ll find answers to frequently asked questions about managing data during a location closure, with helpful links to guide you through the related features and processes.

What happens when I deactivate a location?

When you deactivate a location, staff will no longer have login access to that location. When performing certain actions, closed locations are hidden from selectable dropdown fields throughout the application, including reports, filters, and lists.

  • Any inventory levels, open tickets, pending purchase orders, etc. will remain and will not be deleted. All location data will stay as long as the database has not been archived or deleted, however, it is not viewable unless the location is made active again.

  • Users can still search the Ticket Queue and Customer List for customers created at the closed location. This is helpful for customers who come in and have a purchase history from the previous location.

How do I deactivate a location and/or user?

Admin users in RepairQ can manage active locations and users.

  • For information on deactivating a location, see Adding and Deactivating a Location.

  • For information on deactivating a user, see Staff (User) Management.

I have deactivated a location and/or user. Will my billing automatically update?

No. After deactivating a location/user, you will need to update your subscription in your Company Account settings to reflect this change.

For more information on updating your subscription, check out our article, RepairQ Subscription Management: Changing Your Subscription.

If I close (deactivate) a location, will I lose my data?

No. Deactivating a single location will only remove it from view on dropdown lists and filters. You can reactivate a location if you continue with an active subscription. If you cancel your subscription, your database will be archived and eventually deleted, as part of our Data Retention Policy.

For more information on our data retention process, check out the related section of our article, RepairQ Subscription Management: Read-Only Access.

How can I export my records?

Admins/Managers can export individual reports and list data from customers, inventory, purchase orders, and more. Information is exported by a visible button on the report, or viewing available actions on lists.

For more information on exporting, check out our article, Exporting Data from RepairQ.

Can I temporarily pause my account/locations?

Yes, however, there is still a monthly charge for maintaining the database. Our Read-Only plan starts at $25/month per active location. Read-Only status affects all locations on the database. Unfortunately, there is no way to make a single location Read-Only while other locations remain active.

For more information on Read-Only Rates and Features, check out our article, RepairQ Subscription Management: Read-Only Access.

Can I reactivate a location at a later time?

Yes. You must check your Plan Details to ensure you have an available location. If the details show no available location, you must update your plan for the additional location slot, and then reactivate the location in Company Settings.

For more information on reactivating a location check out the related section of our article, Adding and Deactivating a Location.

If a customer purchased from a location that is now closed, can I return that item to another store?

Yes. Use the Global Search Bar to search by the Ticket ID, or filter the Customer List to find the User Profile. Follow the standard Return Process to return any items and perform a refund.

After returning an item, a new Inventory Record and Change Log is created. The Returned Items section of the ticket has a link to view the inventory item and history. The Ticket Items section of the ticket has a link to the previous Change Log for the sold item.

Can I still access Customer Profiles created from a deactivated location?

Yes. Users can still view Customer Profiles created from a closed location. When filtering the Customer List, select the “Search All Locations” filter to search all locations, including inactive.

Note: This filter differs from the Location Filter, which searches only the locations where you have a role assignment. See Customer Search for more info.

If I close a location, can I still view the System Log details for that location?

The System Log records user activity and does not list the location where the change was made. The log can be filtered for any changes made by the user and location, however, a deactivated location will not show in the dropdown list.

The System Log will show changes made by a user only if they had a role assigned to a location before closure. For instance, if a user has a role at Store A and Store B, and Store B closes, their activity is still searchable by their name and by selecting Any from the location filter. This information cannot be viewed if the user’s role for that location was removed before closing.

How can I manage the inventory at a closing location?

Do a Final Inventory Count

We recommend doing a final inventory count at your location before marking it as inactive. Check out Inventory Count Process: Resources for a list of helpful articles to guide you through this process.

Inventory Valuation Snapshot

After performing an inventory count, we recommend capturing an Inventory Valuation Snapshot to ensure you have this information ahead of time for tax season in the following year.

Transfer Inventory to an Active Location

Once all inventory has been accounted for, these items can be moved to another location.

  • Bulk-Edit Inventory Item Attributes - An Admin can filter the Inventory Summary List for Instock items and update the location assignment through the bulk edit process. This will immediately reassign those items to the location of your choice. See Inventory Management: Editing Inventory Attributes for more info.

  • Inventory Transfer - Transfer your inventory via Purchase Orders to other locations in your database. For more information, see Requesting Items From Another Location.

Void Inventory Associated with Inactive Catalog Items

Make sure that all instock quantities associated with inactive catalog items are purged from your inventory. Marking a Catalog Item inactive does not remove it from inventory, so we recommend adjusting those quantities out. for more information, see Inventory Management: Void Inventory Associated with Inactive Catalog Items.
