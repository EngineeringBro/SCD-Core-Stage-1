The Qsite Ticket Approvals feature helps you request and record consumer consent for a repair. You can configure your requirements and then request the customer's consent before or during the repair. Approval requests can be sent out when a ticket is placed into your status of choice, and you can customize how you want to deliver the request to the customer. Consumers can view, make notes, and accept or reject your request for approval. Once accepted, your ticket status will update, allowing your staff to continue with approved repairs.

After establishing the basic settings and conditions for your approval process, the next step is to set up customer Approval Notifications.

Approval Notifications

For your customers to be notified that there is an Approval waiting for their acceptance, you'll need to set up and configure your automated notifications.

1. On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings.

2. On the Settings Overview page, look for the Communications section. Under this category, click Notifications.

3. When viewing the Notification Automation page, we have created several standard Approval Notifications for your environment.

  • Ticket Approval Create - This email will be delivered to the address on file for the Customer attached to the ticket, notifying them there is a pending approval request.

  • Ticket Approval Accept - This email will be delivered to the address on file for the Location in RepairQ, notifying you of an approval response submitted by the customer.

  • Ticket Approval Reject - This email will be delivered to the address on file for the Location in RepairQ, notifying you of a rejection response submitted by the customer.

Customize your Approval Notifications

If you wish to customize the Notification Name, Location scope, and other fields. Click the Edit button next to the "Ticket Approval Create" notification.

Note: For this notification to work as intended, the trigger and action should remain Trigger - Ticket Approval and Approval Actions - Create.  You can add more triggers based on ticket types and other parameters.

Use the shortcodes in the email message sections to modify the Message Subject, Send To, Reply-To, and Email Message fields.

Note: The only delivery method supported for Ticket Approval Notifications is by Email, so keep that set.

In the Email Message field, there is a default message with a link that reads, "Ticket Approval Portal", which is hyperlinked to the {qsite_approval_url} shortcode. That URL will be created with an embedded token for each Ticket Approval. When that Approval is created (after the ticket is marked as Pending Approval, etc.) the unique URL will be generated and delivered via the link in this notification.

After you have made your changes, be sure to click Save at the bottom of the page to submit your changes. Once you have set up your notifications, you are ready to send and finalize the Approval Request.
