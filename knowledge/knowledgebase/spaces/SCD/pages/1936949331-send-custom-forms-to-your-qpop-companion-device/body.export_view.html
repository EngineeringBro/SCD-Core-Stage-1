Qpop is a customer-facing application designed to enhance interaction and engagement at the point of sale through a dedicated touchscreen interface. Always facing the customer, Qpop allows for seamless interaction during the sales process by:

  • Displaying in-store advertisements and promotions

  • Showing real-time ticket details for customer review

  • Presenting electronic forms and waivers for acknowledgment

  • Capturing electronic signatures for payments via integrated devices

Qpop Companion also works with Custom Forms, allowing customers to read and sign repair waivers, terms and conditions, disclaimers, and more.

Add ‘Send to Qpop’ to Existing Custom Forms

On your terminal, use the Navigation Bar to go to Settings → Location Settings. On the Settings Overview page, under the Tickets section, select Custom Forms.

Note: You can change settings for all locations by selecting Company Settings.

On the Custom Forms page, locate the form you would like to push to Qpop. Under the Actions column, click Edit. Near the bottom of the field settings, there are additional selections for Qpop behavior. When you are satisfied with your selections, click Save field group settings to submit your changes.

  • Qpop enabled - Select this option to push the form to Qpop manually.

  • Send to Qpop automatically when required - Select this option to send the form automatically during the ticket flow.

  • Require Signature on Submit - Select this option to capture the customer's signature on your forms.

Send a Custom Form to Qpop

When editing a ticket, scroll to the Custom Forms section. Locate the form you would like to push to Qpop companion. Under the Actions column, click the Send to Qpop button. The form will automatically send to your Qpop Companion for your customer to read, fill out, and acknowledge/sign, directly on the Qpop device. After your customer has acknowledged the form, tap Submit to finalize the form.

After the form is submitted, your terminal will notify you that the form is complete and ready to be saved. You can continue the ticket as normal. Save the ticket by either progressing to a new status or clicking Save.

Print a Signed Form

Custom Form Settings

When editing your form fields, make sure that Print Visible is selected to print a copy of the signed form for customer records.

View/Print Actions on Tickets

When viewing a ticket with submitted forms, Admins and Managers can choose whether to view or print the form.

  • Magnifying Glass Icon - View the signed form (with “Signed Electronically” as the signature).

  • Printer Icon - Print a copy of the signed form (with “Signed Electronically” as the signature).

  • Completed Form Icon - View and print a copy of the form with the visible signature.
