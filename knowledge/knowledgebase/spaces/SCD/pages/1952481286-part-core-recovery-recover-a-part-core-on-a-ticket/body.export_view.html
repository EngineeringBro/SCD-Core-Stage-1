Part Core Recovery allows companies to recover and track parts that can be sent back to the manufacturer for reimbursement or credit. Part Cores may include screens, batteries, or other assorted parts that can be remanufactured or recycled for their materials.

This process allows stores to reduce waste and increase their margins through discounts and credits. When a catalog item is marked as recoverable and you have activated Part Core Recovery, the bad/broken core is added to inventory as recovered. Then it can be sent back via the RMA return process. When a recoverable part is added to a ticket, and the ticket has been placed into the appropriate status, the Part Recovery window will appear to record the part core information.

Recovering a Part Core on a Ticket

When a recoverable part is added to a ticket, and the ticket has been placed into the appropriate status, the Part Recovery window will appear. The window will notify the user that the item should be recovered, and you will need to fill out the information for the recovered part. Details for the New Part are on the left column, and the details for the Recovered Part are on the right.

  • Part not recoverable - Select this box if the part is not able to be recovered. You will need to provide a reason why the part cannot be returned. The selections are determined by your Part Recovery Reasons list.

  • Serial - (Conditional) If you are working with a serialized item, you will need to enter the serial number of the returned part.

  • Serial not accessible - (Conditional) If you are unable to obtain a serial number for the item, selecting this box will auto-generate a serial number after saving.

    • Note: You must have the auto-serialize feature set up and active for this to work properly.

  • Grade - Select a grade of the recovered part, The selections are determined by your Part Recovery Grade list.

  • Condition - Select a condition of the recovered part, based on the selections available in the dropdown. New conditions/groups can be created in the Catalog section of Company Settings.

  • Notes - Add any optional notes for the recovered part.

When you are finished filling out the part details, click Continue with Recovery.

After you finish the recovery process, the item will be listed under the Pending RMA Items section of the ticket. The item will be placed in Pending RMA status and is ready to be sent back to the supplier. for more information on this process, check out our article, RMA Process: Sending RMA Items to the Supplier (RMA Batch).
