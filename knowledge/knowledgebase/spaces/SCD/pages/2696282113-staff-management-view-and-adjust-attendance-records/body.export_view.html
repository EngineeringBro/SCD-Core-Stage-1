Accurate time tracking ensures you have a clear view of labor costs, employee hours, and overtime, helping you manage your team efficiently while keeping payroll accurate. RepairQ simplifies attendance management with built-in tools for clocking in and out, adding or editing time records, and monitoring overtime with helpful notifications. With these features, you can focus on running your business while ensuring your staff's time is accounted for.

  • Clock-In and Clock-Out - Staff can easily record their work hours directly within RepairQ.

  • Attendance Monitoring - View live attendance records to track who's working, late, or absent at any location.

  • Overtime Management and Alerts - Receive dashboard notifications for overtime hours to control labor costs and comply with labor regulations.

  • Add or Edit Time Records - Add or adjust time entries to ensure accurate payroll and attendance logs.

View Timecard Records

Users with the Shift Lead role or higher can access attendance records to monitor staff hours. To view attendance, navigate to Staff → Attendance from the navigation bar.

The Attendance page displays a weekly view by default, showing the current week. Users can:

  • Navigate to previous weeks using the controls.

  • Filter attendance records by date range and other criteria.

  • Export attendance reports for further analysis or record-keeping.

Duration Calculation

RepairQ uses decimal time to represent portions of an hour, converting minutes into a decimal format for timekeeping and reporting. This format simplifies calculations for payroll and other processes. For example:

  • 7 hours and 5 minutes is represented as 7.08

  • 7 hours and 10 minutes is represented as 7.17

  • 7 hours and 15 minutes is represented as 7.25

  • 7 hours and 20 minutes is represented as 7.33

  • 7 hours and 25 minutes is represented as 7.42

  • 7 hours and 30 minutes is represented as 7.50

Edit a Timecard Record

Users with Manager or Admin access can edit timecard records directly from the Attendance page. Navigate to Staff → Attendance and use the available filters to locate the staff member and specific day you need to update. Once you’ve found the correct entry, highlight the hours for that day to access options to Edit or Delete the time entry. This ensures timekeeping records remain accurate and up-to-date.

Note: Any days that exceed 8 worked hours and weekly totals that exceed 40 hours are highlighted in yellow.

On the Edit screen, you can update the location, date, and time for the selected entry. Be sure to use a 24-hour format for timekeeping. After adjusting, be sure to Save your changes.

Add a Timecard Record

Users with Manager or Admin access can edit timecard records directly from the Attendance page. Navigate to Staff → Attendance and click +Add New Time Record.

On the New Time Entry screen, select the Location and Employee for whom you need to adjust, then choose the date of the adjustment. Enter the start and end times in 24-hour format. If there are split work hours (such as for lunch or multiple shifts), you may need to create multiple entries to accurately reflect the worked hours. Be sure to Save your changes.

Timecard Changes and the System Log

All timecard changes are logged in the System Log to ensure transparency and maintain timecard integrity. You can filter results by Item: Timesheet to view related entries.
