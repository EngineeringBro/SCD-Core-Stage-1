The Tip Report in RepairQ is designed to help you track all tips received and refunded during transactions. This ensures a clear record of all tip-related activity, which is important for financial management and compliance with regulations. Directing tip records to your payroll software ensures that all tip income is correctly reported and processed, making payroll management smoother and more accurate.

Key Features

  • Tip Amounts - View all tips received or refunded from transactions.

  • Filters - Search records for ticket dates and statuses to refine the report.

  • Staff Assignments - The report shows which staff member records a tip.

Tip Guidelines

  • Integrated Payments - Tips are limited to our Square and Bluefin Integrated Payments solutions.

  • VCT Reconciliation - Tips are recorded with integrated card transactions and contribute to the nightly cash register balance process.

  • Tip Distribution - For reporting and distributing tips/tip refunds, many payroll software solutions include features to track and report tips as part of payroll processing. This ensures compliance with IRS regulations regarding tip income.

  • Tip Refunds - RepairQ records tip refunds, however, it is the responsibility of store management/operations to handle these payout situations.

Viewing the Tip Report

On the Navigation bar, click Reports. When viewing the Report page, the Tip Report is listed under “Tip Amount.”

Tip Report Detail

Use the filters to find transactions within a certain range, location, and status. Once you applied the appropriate filters, click Apply Filters to see the results.

  • Date - This is the date of the transaction recorded on a ticket.

  • Location - This is the location where the transaction was taken.

  • Ticket Date - This is the date the ticket was last updated. Ticket Date is subject to change until the ticket is closed.

  • Status - This will scope results for the following ticket statuses: All Tickets, Closed Tickets Only, and Open Tickets Only.

  • Billing Agent - This will scope results for tickets assigned to the selected billing agent(s).

Tip Report Columns

  • Note - The Reference#, Note, or Last #4 of the card used for the transaction.

  • Date - The recorded transaction date

  • Ticket Date - The date the ticket was last updated. This is subject to change until the ticket is closed.

  • Ticket # - The Ticket ID#

  • Ticket Status - The current ticket status

  • Payment Method - The payment method used for the transaction

  • Billing Agent - The assigned Billing Agent for the ticket

  • Location - The location where the payment was received

  • Terminal - The VCT/Register that took the payment

  • Added By - The user who logged the transaction

  • Tip Amount - The amount tipped during payment

Exporting the Tip Report

You can show/hide the filter section of the Tip report and export the report in Excel format (.xlsx) in the upper, right corner of the page.

Tip Distribution

For reporting and distributing tips/tip refunds, many payroll software solutions include features to track and report tips as part of payroll processing. This ensures compliance with IRS regulations regarding tip income.

Note: We recommend using information from Closed tickets following our Accounting Principles.

Tip Pooling

Tip pooling involves collecting all earned tips and then redistributing them according to a pre-determined formula. This practice is common in environments where teamwork significantly contributes to service quality.

  • Transparency: Communicate to all employees, including how tips are collected and redistributed (timeframe, payout method, etc.).

  • Fair Distribution: Ensure the distribution formula is equitable and considers factors such as job role, hours worked, and contribution to the team.

  • Compliance: Adhere to local labor laws and regulations regarding tip pooling.

  • Tip Report - Run the report for a Ticket Date range (daily, weekly, etc.) and Tickets in Closed Status. Total all received tips and record them in your accounting software for distribution.

Direct Tip Distribution

Direct tip distribution means tips are given directly to the employee who received them. This practice is often used in businesses where individual performance directly impacts the tips received.

  • Transparency: Communicate to all employees, including how tips are collected and redistributed (timeframe, payout method, etc.).

  • Ticket Assignee - You can require an assignee for Repair Tickets and which ticket statuses require a resolution.

  • Ticket Item Assignee - The “Sold By” field is assigned to the user who gets credit for the sold ticket item. The “Repaired By” field is assigned to the user who repairs an item. These assignments are reflected in the Sales Detail by Staff and Repairs by Tech Reports, respectively.

  • Tip Report - Run the report for a Ticket Date range (daily, weekly, etc.) and Tickets in Closed Status. If you are distributing tickets by Ticket Assignee or Ticket Item Assignee, use the link provided for each line item to view the ticket information for the staff who repaired/sold the items. Record this info in your accounting software for distribution.
