The Ticket Resolution feature allows you to add key details to Repair and Refurb tickets, improving classification, reporting, and filtering.

You can also require a Resolution assignment before saving tickets in new statuses, ensuring accurate tracking, reporting, and quality control. Resolutions can be set for specific ticket types and statuses, with Admins managing them across all locations and Managers creating them for individual locations.

  • Enhanced Classification and Reporting - Add resolutions for better ticket classification and reporting.

  • Mandatory Resolutions - Enforce Ticket Resolutions for specific statuses and ticket types.

  • Custom Forms Integration - Works with custom forms and notifications.

Ticket Requirements

Log into RepairQ as an Admin. On the RepairQ Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings. On the Settings Overview page, under the Tickets section, click General Settings.

Note: To create a Resolution Type scoped to your specific location, navigate to Settings → Location Settings.

Status Advancement Required Fields and Blockers

On the Ticket General Settings page, under the Ticket Resolution category, activate the Required When: setting. In the text boxes, select the Ticket Statuses and Ticket Types that will require a resolution. Once your selections are made, click the Save button in the upper-right corner to apply your changes.

Locating the Resolution Field on a Ticket

The Resolution field is located in the top row of selections on a Repair or Refurb ticket. A resolution must be selected before advancing the ticket to the designated status.

If the Resolution field has not been selected, the user will be prompted to select a Resolution before progressing to the selected ticket status.
