The Qsite Ticket Approvals feature helps you request and record consumer consent for a repair. You can configure your requirements to request the customer's consent before or during the repair. Approval requests can be sent out when a ticket is placed into Pending Approval status, and you can customize how you want to deliver the request to the customer. Consumers can view, make notes, and accept or reject your request for approval. After acceptance, the ticket status will update, allowing your staff to continue with approved repairs.

The first step is establishing basic settings and conditions for your approval process.

Ticket Approval Settings

1. On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings.

2. On the Settings Overview screen, scroll down to the Qsite section and click Ticket Approval.

3. On the Ticket Approval page, change the toggle to Enabled. Next, begin filling out the parameters of the approval request.

  • Title - Assign a title to this approval. The customer will see this information, so title it accordingly. For example, "Repair Approval Request."

  • Description - This is the message your customer will receive regarding the approval request.

4. Fill out the Accepted/Declined confirmation page details. You can assign a title and description to both responses. Customers will see a confirmation screen when they accept or decline the approval.

  • Accepted Confirmation

    • Title - This is the title of your approval confirmation screen.

    • Description - This is the message your customer sees upon approval.

  • Declined Confirmation

    • Title - This is the title of your declined confirmation screen.

    • Description - This is the message your customer sees upon declination.

5. (Optional) Establish the approval terms for your request. These terms are secondary to your standard warranty policy. When enabled, these terms are visible to the customer when they accept/decline the approval request.

  • Toggle - Use this to enable/disable approval terms.

  • Text Field - Add any additional terms and conditions for approvals.

6. (Optional) Decide if the customer will be required to view ticket details (services, parts, pricing, etc.) before accepting or declining the request.

  • Toggle - Use this to enable/disable the detail view requirement for customers.

  • Title - This is the title to direct the customer to view ticket details.

  • Description - This is the message your customer will see when requesting to view ticket details.

7. Select if/when a customer follow-up note is required upon submission. After you have made your selections, be sure to click Save to update your settings.

  • Never - The customer will not be required to provide a note, regardless of the response.

  • On Reject - The customer must provide a note upon declining approval.

  • On Accept - The customer must provide a note upon accepting approval.

  • Always Require a note - The customer must provide a note, regardless of the response.

  • Title - This will direct the customer to provide additional details.

  • Description - This is the message your customer will see when requesting additional details.

Ticket Approval Conditions

Approvals will be required when tickets enter a specific status. Approval requests will also expire after a set amount of time.

1. On the Navigation Bar, go to Company Settings. On the Settings Overview page, click the Tickets dropdown on the left-hand column, and from the list of options, select Approvals.

2. Set your parameters for Ticket Approval requests on the Ticket Approval page. After you have made your selections, click Save Settings to submit your changes.

  • Approval Request Expiration - Assign a timer, in minutes, for how long an approval request will remain active. For example, if you want the approval request to expire after 24 hours, you would enter 1440 minutes; 48 hours would be 2880 minutes.

  • Statuses - Select which ticket status(es) will require a customer to approve their ticket before you can proceed. Click inside the box to select/deselect the statuses of your choice. Remember that any time a ticket is saved into an assigned status, you will be required to obtain approval from the customer.

  • Actions - (Optional) Select which ticket actions will require a customer to approve the ticket before you can proceed. The same behaviors apply as noted above, similar to statuses.
