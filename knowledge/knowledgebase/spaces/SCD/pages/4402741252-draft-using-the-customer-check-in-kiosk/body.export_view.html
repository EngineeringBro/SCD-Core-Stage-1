Using the Customer Check-in Kiosk

The Customer Check-in Kiosk lets customers add themselves to your location's service queue before a staff member is available. Walk-in customers fill in their contact information, and customers with appointments can verify and confirm their booking — all from a self-service QPOP device. Staff see the live queue on their RepairQ dashboard and can create tickets directly from it.

Before You Start

For customers using the kiosk:

  • A QPOP device must be set up and displaying the kiosk screen at your location

  • No login or account is required

For staff managing the queue:

  • The Customer Check-in Kiosk feature must be enabled for your location

  • You must be logged in to RepairQ

Section 1: Checking In as a Customer

Use the kiosk if you've walked in without an appointment, or if you have a scheduled appointment and want to let staff know you've arrived.

1. Tap Check In on the Kiosk

Approach the QPOP kiosk device. On the screen, tap Check In.

Note: If the screen is showing promotional messages, tap anywhere to return to the main screen, then tap Check In.

<!-- screenshot: step-01-kiosk-checkin-button.png -->

2. Choose Your Check-in Type

Select how you're visiting today:

  • Tap Walk-in if you don't have a scheduled appointment

  • Tap I Have an Appointment if you booked in advance

<!-- screenshot: step-02-checkin-type-selection.png -->

Walk-in Check-in

3. Enter Your Information

Fill in the following fields:

  • First Name (required)

  • Last Name (required)

  • Phone Number (required)

  • Email (optional)

When finished, tap Submit.

<!-- screenshot: step-03-walkin-form.png -->

4. Confirm You're in the Queue

A confirmation screen appears letting you know you're in the queue. Your position is displayed so you know where you stand. Take a seat — a staff member will call your name when they're ready.

<!-- screenshot: step-04-walkin-confirmation.png -->

Appointment Check-in

3. Enter Your Appointment Details

Enter the following to look up your booking:

  • Phone Number

  • Last Name

Tap Look Up My Appointment.

<!-- screenshot: step-03-appointment-lookup.png -->

4. Confirm Your Appointment

If your appointment is found, your booking details (date, time, and service) appear on screen. Review the details to make sure everything looks correct, then tap Confirm Check-In.

Tip: If your appointment isn't found, you'll have the option to try again with a different phone number or last name. If it still can't be found, tap Continue Anyway — a staff member will confirm your appointment when they assist you.

<!-- screenshot: step-04-appointment-confirm.png -->

5. You're Checked In

A confirmation screen confirms you're now checked in. Staff have been notified of your arrival. Take a seat and a staff member will be with you shortly.

<!-- screenshot: step-05-appointment-confirmation.png -->

Section 2: Managing the Queue (Staff)

When customers check in via the kiosk, they appear in the Customer Queue widget on your RepairQ dashboard. Use this widget to monitor who's waiting and to get started on their ticket.

1. Open the Customer Queue Widget

From the RepairQ Dashboard, locate the Customer Queue widget. The widget is only visible when at least one customer has checked in.

The queue displays up to 10 customers at a time, showing:

  • First name and last initial

  • Order of check-in (first arrived shown at the top)

<!-- screenshot: step-06-staff-queue-widget.png -->

2. Review a Customer Entry

Locate the customer you're ready to assist. Each entry shows the customer's name and check-in time. Click the entry to see the available actions.

<!-- screenshot: step-07-customer-entry-actions.png -->

3. Select an Action

The following actions are available for each queue entry:

Action

What it does

View

Opens the customer's full details

Create Ticket

Opens ticket creation with customer info pre-filled

Remove

Manually removes the customer from the queue

Add to Queue

Manually adds a customer who hasn't checked in

Note: For customers who checked in with an appointment, ticket creation options are hidden. You'll be asked to confirm before removing them from the queue.

4. Create a Ticket from the Queue

Click Create Ticket on the customer's entry. Select the ticket type:

  • Sale

  • Repair

  • Trade-in

<!-- screenshot: step-08-create-ticket-type.png -->

5. Complete the Ticket

The ticket creation screen opens with the customer's name and contact information already filled in. Complete any remaining fields and click Save.

<!-- screenshot: step-09-ticket-creation-prefilled.png -->

6. Customer is Removed from the Queue

Once the ticket is saved, the customer is automatically removed from the Customer Queue widget. No manual removal is needed.

<!-- screenshot: step-10-queue-updated.png -->

Result

Customers receive a confirmation on the kiosk showing they're in the queue or checked in for their appointment.

Staff can see the live queue on their dashboard and create tickets with pre-filled customer information — reducing manual data entry and keeping the service flow moving.
