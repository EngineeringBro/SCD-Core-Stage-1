RepairQ utilizes a Virtual Cash Terminal (VCT) to record ticket transactions so you can reconcile your cash flow at the end of the day. Users can create custom payment methods tailored to their business needs, providing enhanced flexibility, accuracy, and control over the transaction management processes.

  • Customization - Define payment methods that align with your business model and customer preferences.

  • Accuracy -Ensure precise recording of various payment types, facilitating accurate financial reporting.

Creating a New Payment Method

1. On the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings. For managers of a location, log into the location you are adding the payment method, then go to Settings → Location Settings.

2. On the Settings Overview screen, under the Payments section, click Payment Methods.

3. On the overview page, you’ll see a list of default methods. Click Add Payment Method on the right side.

4. Begin filling out the fields for the new payment method. When you are satisfied with your settings, click Save.

  • Location - Payment Methods added at the company level will apply to all locations by default. If you manage multiple locations, you can select which location to add the payment method or select all locations.

  • Name - Assign a name to identify your payment method. For example, "CashApp."

  • Type - Select a payment type that corresponds with the payment method.

  • Gateway (optional) - If you select the Credit Card payment type, you’ll be prompted to select a gateway. If you are not using our integrated payment feature, select “Non-integrated.”

  • Payments Account (optional) - If this payment method requires a different account than you have configured in your Journal Entry Classifications, you can assign it here.

  • Priority (optional) - This determines the order of Payment Methods on the Overview screen. You can assign a number, beginning at zero, to rearrange the order of methods. Lower numbers will sit higher on the list.

  • Is Active - Check this box to activate the payment method. To deactivate the payment method, uncheck the box.

5. The new payment method will show on the Payment Method Overview screen after saving your settings.

Note: Unused payment methods can be deleted, however, once linked to a payment on a ticket, it cannot be deleted, to maintain data integrity.
