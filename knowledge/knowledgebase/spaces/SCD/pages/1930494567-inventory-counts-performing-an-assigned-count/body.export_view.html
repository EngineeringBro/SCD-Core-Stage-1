Assigned counts enable managers to delegate inventory count tasks to specific staff members, allowing for a focused audit of different divisions within your store's inventory. Managers can easily assign these counts, set due dates, and manage conflicts with ongoing counts. This method is well-suited for routine inventory management, such as monthly counts. After the counts are completed and submitted by staff, an Inventory Manager or higher can review the results, reconcile any discrepancies, and make necessary adjustments before finalizing the inventory.

Staff can perform their assigned counts two ways: Individual line item counts or batch counting. The processes below will guide a Staff Member through their count after a manager has created the assignment.

Counts by Line Item

From the RepairQ Navigation Bar, go to Inventory → Inventory Counts to view your assignments. On the Inventory Counts summary page, you will see a list of current counts assigned to you. Items that have not been counted will be in Counting status.

Find the item that you would like to begin counting from the list. Under the Actions column, click the Options (⋮) button to expand additional actions, and click Count to begin the count for this item.

A pop-up will appear with the name of the item you are currently counting. If you are utilizing a scanner, you can scan the item's barcode, or increase the Count quantity for non-serialized items,

Counting Serialized Items

When counting serialized items, you must scan or enter the serial numbers found for the item. Multiple serial numbers can be entered by listing each number on separate lines in the Serials field. Click Save Count to submit your items as Counted status, ready for a manager to finalize.

Non-Serialized Items

Scan the items or enter a count total for the items. After you are finished counting, click Save Count to submit your items as Counted status, ready for a manager to finalize.

Counts by Batch

Batch counts let you count large groups of assigned inventory items at once. Batch counts are managed from the Count Sheet. Below is a breakdown of the different sections of the Count Sheet.

From the RepairQ Navigation Bar, go to Inventory → Inventory Counts. On the Inventory Counts Summary page, view the Count Sheet by clicking the Count button near the top-right of the page.

Inventory Count Sheet

On the Count Sheet, click Load Assignments on the upper-right part of the page. This will list all counts assigned to you in the Counted Items section.

  • Scan/Search To Count - This field can be toggled to accept scanned barcodes and serial numbers or to keyword search items by name, SKU, etc. Items scanned/searched here will update the Current Item section.

  • Current Item - This shows the most recently counted item and allows you to adjust the count total or view counted serial numbers.

    • If the Current Item is non-serialized, you can update the count total to match the found amount and click Save Count to update the total for the Counted Items list or Cancel to remove the current item count.

    • If the Current Item is serialized, you can view a list of all counted serial numbers so far. Once you have finished entering serial numbers for this item from the search field, click Save Count to update the Counted Items list or Cancel to remove the current item count.

  • Counted Items- Lists items in Counted status, their current count total, and available actions.

    • View Serials - View a list of serial numbers recorded for this item.

    • Edit - Updates the Current Item field to allow editing options for the selected item.

    • Remove - Removes the item from the Counted Items list.

    • Save for Later/Save & Submit - Save your progress to submit later, or submit the current count of items from the Counted Item list. Submitting the list will move these items into Counted status, ready for a Manager to make adjustments and reconcile.

  • Count Log - Lists scan entries, successful counts, warnings, error messages, etc. Warnings are highlighted in yellow, and errors are highlighted in red.

Counting Items From the Batch Sheet

There are two ways to add items to your Counted Items list: Scan Mode or Lookup Mode. You can toggle between modes by clicking the Search Type icon next to the search field.

Scan Mode

Scan SKU/UPC/Serial Number barcodes. When a barcode is scanned or entered, it is automatically added to the Current Item count.

Lookup Mode

Search for items by their keyword, SKU, or serial number to add to the Current Items section of the Count Sheet.

Counts will update according to the Current Item listed on the Count Sheet. Non-serialized item counts will increase as the item is submitted. Make sure to click Save Count to update your current total before counting a different type of item.

Adding Missing Serialized Items

If you come across a serialized item that isn’t in your inventory during the count, you can easily add it. First, click the Edit button next to the item in the Counted Items list to refresh the Current Item section. Then, scan or type the serial number to include the item in your count. Once the item is counted, click Save Count to update the total in the Counted Items list.

As items are added to the Count Sheet, a Log is kept of your count activity, the Current Item you are counting, and a Counted Item list of the items that have been tallied so far.

Continue counting your inventory by scanning or adding items to the list. During your count, you may need to pause your activity. Click Save for Later to save your current counts and finish later.

If you are finished counting all items, click Save & Submit items to Counted status for a manager to finalize and reconcile the count.

Counted Status

Once you have saved the count, the item will be listed in Counted status on the Inventory Count page. Under the Counted column, you will see the total you have submitted for reconciliation.

Note: Because we utilize a Blind Count method, you may not see totals in the In Stock, Variance, Reconciled, and other fields. This is expected, so counts cannot be edited to match the current stock value. This information, however, is visible to managers when reconciling the count.

Re-Counting Items

If you need to update/recount any count totals, use the Count button again to change the count total/serial numbers. The count totals or serial numbers you enter will overwrite your current totals. Once an item is Counted, it is ready for a Manager to reconcile the count.
