Occasionally, an item will be defective during initial inspection upon receiving the item into your inventory. A Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA) is a part of the Reverse Logistics cycle, where defective products are returned to the manufacturer or vendor during the warranty period. Once an item has been marked as Pending RMA in the system, the next step is to send the item(s) back to the supplier/vendor for approval. When items have been sent out, they will be marked as RMA Sent in your inventory. This signifies that the item is in transit to the supplier, but the RMA has not yet been accepted. RMA items are monitored on the RMA Tracking page in RepairQ.

Creating an RMA Batch (Shipment)

When sending a shipment (whether one item or a group of items) back to the supplier, this shipment is called a batch. Users can select specific items to send in a batch, or the system can group batches by the supplier. This allows you to quickly create batches based on where you purchased the items.

1. On the Navigation Bar, go to Inventory → RMA Tracking.

2. On the Tracking page, change the filter view to Pending RMA and apply. Items that have been moved to this status will show on the list, with aged items at the bottom.

3. Select the item(s) you wish to send back by clicking the checkbox(es) next to those items on the list. Once you have made your selection(s), click the Edit Selected button. From the list of options, click Send Selected.

4. A Create New RMA Batch pop-up will appear. This will show the item count for all of the items selected for the batch. You can add any additional information for this batch, including an RMA #, Shipper, and Tracking information. If you wish to print a packaging slip for the batch upon creation, click the checkbox under the Print column. Once you have added information and made your selections, click Create New RMA Batches to create the batch and place the items in RMA Sent status.

Once the RMA Batch is created, you can view the details, including the batch number, supporting details, and the items that are part of this batch. You can also use the Options button to open the batch for editing, print another packing slip, or download the information in an Excel-friendly format.

Creating RMA Batches for Items by Supplier

If you are a high volume of items/batches back, you can quick-create batches sorted by their supplier.

When viewing the RMA Tracking List, set the filter status for Pending RMA and Apply. On the upper-right side of the page, click the Options (⋮) button. From the available list of options, select Batch All.

A Create New RMA Batches pop-up will appear. The window will show the Item Count next to each Supplier. One batch will be created for each Supplier on the list. You can add any additional information for each batch, including an RMA #, Shipper, and Tracking information. If you wish to print a packaging slip for the batch upon creation, click the checkbox under the Print column. If you see a batch that you do not want to create at this time, click the Delete button under the Actions column to cancel the batch.

Once you have added information and made your selections, click Create New RMA Batches to create the batch and place the items in RMA Sent status.

Once an RMA batch is created, the inventory items are placed into RMA Sent status, and you will be directed to the RMA Batch index, where you can view individual batch details, print packing slips, or begin the RMA Reconciliation Process.
