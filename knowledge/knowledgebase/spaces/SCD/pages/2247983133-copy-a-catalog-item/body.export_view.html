The Catalog lists all items and services available for your store. Any item that can be purchased, sold, repaired, or used as parts needs a corresponding inventory item. Services and Labor can be added to the Catalog for charging customers for services and reporting the Cost Of Goods Sold (COGS) on repairs.

The field details for adding a Catalog Item may change depending on the customization of your item. For a detailed explanation of these fields, check out our RepairQ Catalog Overview.

Catalog items can also be copied to save you time when creating similar catalog items. A user with the Inventory Manager role or higher can create/copy catalog items.

Copy a Catalog Item

Only items scoped to the Global Level (all locations) can be copied.

When viewing the item you want to copy, click the Options button (⋮) under the Actions column. From the dropdown list of actions, click Copy.

A pop-up will appear that allows you to select which related settings will also copy with the item. When you have made your selection(s) click Copy to create the item.

Note: By default, copying a catalog item will retain all initial field data and selected attributes as the original, but the item will be inactive.

  • Compatibility - Inherit the approved parts and service lists of the original catalog item.

  • Supplier SKUs - Inherit the linked Supplier SKU assigned to the original catalog item.

  • Location Overrides - Inherit the Location Overrides assigned to the original catalog item.

  • Categories - Inherit the Category Tree assignment of the original catalog item.

  • Service Program Catalog Overrides - Inherit the Service Program overrides assigned to the original catalog item.

After copying, you will enter Edit Mode for the new catalog item. The item name will have the text “(Copy-#)” affixed to the name to indicate it is the copied version. The item will also default as inactive, so new tickets are not immediately affected by the new item. Make any adjustments to the field data and attribute selections. Be sure to mark the new item as Active before saving your changes.

Note: Double-check the Behaviors section of the item creation page to change attributes for serialized and inventoried status before saving the entry, as these are locked for editing later.

Locate Copied, Inactive Items

When you copy an item, it creates an inactive catalog entry in Edit mode. If you leave this page before marking the item as Active (and Save your changes), it won’t be immediately accessible in your catalog. If you’ve copied an item and can’t find it in your Catalog List, it’s it may still be inactive.

Filter for Inactive Catalog Items

When viewing your catalog, use the filters to search for Attributes: Inactive and Date Range of your choice with the Date Type: Created Date.

Apply your filters, and you will see any recently created items in Inactive status. Any copies will be identified by the Name of the item. From here, you can edit the item and mark it as Active.

If you find there are unnecessary/duplicate copies of an item, click the Options button (⋮) under the Actions column. From the dropdown list of actions, click Delete.
