Occasionally, an item will be defective during initial inspection or after an installation. A Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA) is a part of the Reverse Logistics cycle, where defective products are returned to the manufacturer or vendor during the warranty period. This allows the vendor to diagnose and correct the issue with the product(s). They may proceed with a refund or replacement if found defective or reject the claim if the product was damaged or broken by misuse or improper installation. Ultimately, the decisions are made at the supplier's discretion to credit these items.

Marking Defective Items as RMA on Open Tickets

To initiate the RMA process, the defective item(s) will need to be set to Pending RMA status. This is a transitional status, meaning that it exists in your inventory, however, can no longer be attached to a ticket. This process can be performed on any ticket type, including Refurb tickets.

1. When viewing the Ticket Items section of a ticket, click the Damaged/Defective (thumb-down) button next to the affected item.

2. A pop-up will appear, where you can add additional details about the defective item. Once you have made your selections, click Update Item to save the item in the appropriate status.

  • Supplier - Select the supplier from where this item was purchased.

  • Inventory Item Note - (Optional) Add any additional notes to assist with the replacement process.

  • Inventory Status - Marking the item as Defective will place the item in Pending RMA status, ready for further attention. Marking an item as Damaged will place the item in Damaged status, meaning the part was damaged and does not qualify for replacement.

3. The item will remain on the ticket as Defective status, but it will not affect the balance. Once you update/save the ticket, the item will be placed in Pending RMA status. On the Ticket View page, the Pending RMA Items section will list any defective items on the ticket.

A Manager can select an RMA item and click the Print Labels button to print an inventory label for the item(s).

Click the View button to see the Item Details page to view the Change Log or edit the inventory item.

The Ticket History will show which staff member marked the item as Pending RMA.

Marking Defective Items as RMA on Closed Tickets (Return)

During the Return Process, a returned item can be placed directly into Pending RMA status. After selecting the item to return, you will choose where to send the item. Under the Initial Diagnostics section, Select Defective (Pending RMA) and continue with the return. When you save the ticket, the item will update to the appropriate status.
