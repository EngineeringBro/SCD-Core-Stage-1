By default, RepairQ includes a built-in notification trigger for manually sending SMS status details from the customer’s ticket. This feature ensures that customers receive essential information, enhancing communication and customer satisfaction. To send an SMS notification, the following must be met:

  • A Customer Profile must be linked to the ticket.

  • The Customer Profile must have a valid mobile phone number added to the ‘SMS Phone’ field, and the correct ‘SMS Carrier’ must be selected.

  • The option ‘Receive Service SMS Notifications’ must be selected in the Customer Profile.

Automated Notifications are intended for transactional and relationship messaging (ticket updates, pickup notifications, internal alerts), not for marketing or promotional content.

For details and regulatory context, see Getting Started with Automated Notifications

Send a Ticket Status SMS

On the Navigation Bar, go to Tickets. When viewing the Ticket Queue, you can use the filters to search for an existing ticket or create a new one.

Link a Customer to a Ticket (New Tickets)

Before saving a ticket or sending a notification, you must attach a customer to the ticket. Use the Attach Customer button to add an existing customer or create a new Customer Profile.

Note: The customer’s profile must have a valid Mobile Number and Carrier, and ‘Receive Service SMS Notifications’ must be active for the customer to receive SMS notifications.

Customer Details on Tickets (Ticket View Mode)

The ‘Customer’ section lists the customer’s contact info. The customer must have a valid SMS Phone Number and Carrier for SMS notifications to be sent successfully.

If the Mobile Number address is missing, or you need to update their notification settings, you can edit the ticket and click the Edit Customer button to add the missing details.

Ticket actions are located in the upper-right corner of the ticket. To send an SMS to the customer with their ticket status, click the Bell Icon. You will receive a notification that the email was successfully queued.

Notification Queue

To check your outgoing notification queue, use the Navigation Bar to go to Reports → Notifications.

On the Notifications Summary page, you can view a queue of all outgoing notifications.

The table includes columns to help you identify the following details:

  • Type - Indicates the type of notification sent. This can either be an automated notification or an announcement.

  • Subject - Displays the subject or title of the outgoing notification.

  • Message - Shows a preview of the main content of the enclosed message.

  • Attachment - Lists any attached files, such as receipts, invoices, etc.

  • Status - Indicates whether the notification is New (pending) or Sent. Notifications have an approximate 5-minute delay before sending, during which you can cancel a queued message by clicking the Red X Button next to the notification.

  • To - Displays the recipient’s email address or SMS phone number.

  • Created At - A timestamp indicating when the notification was queued.

  • Send At - For most notifications, this will show an immediate send time. However, automated notifications may be sent on a delay based on the configured settings.

  • Location - Displays the location that sent the notification.
