RepairQ allows users to attach, or bundle, items within a ticket. When an item is bundled under another item on a ticket, the price of the bundled item does not contribute to the overall ticket total. Instead, the cost of the bundled item, referred to as the Child Item, becomes integrated into the existing parent item’s price.

To ensure that a part is bundled under a labor/service, you can flag the Parent Item to require a bundled item before saving the ticket. You can also assign specific ticket statuses that restrict advancement until an item flagged as “Bundle Required” is added to the ticket. This ensures that tickets cannot advance until all required labor and parts have been bundled.

Bundle Required Item- If a Catalog Item is flagged with “Bundle Required,” a Child Item must be bundled under it to save the ticket. The Parent Item must have the following attributes for a bundle requirement:

  • Create or update an existing Item Type with Labor and Bundle (as parent) attributes.

  • Enable the Bundle Required attribute for the Catalog Item. This enforces the bundling requirement.

Bundle Required Ticket Status - The ticket cannot advance to the assigned status unless an item flagged as “Bundle Required” is added to the ticket.

  • If you require a bundle for a Ticket Status, this is managed in Ticket General Settings.

  • Bundle requirements on tickets are managed at the Company or Location level.

Verify the Item Type is Compatible with Bundle Required

An Admin will need to log into your RepairQ environment to confirm this. From the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings. On the Company Settings Overview page, scroll down to the Catalog section and click on Item Types.

At your discretion, edit an existing Item Type, and apply the Labor and Bundle (as parent) attributes.

Note: You may also decide to create a new Item Type for this purpose. If you create a new type, don’t forget to an applicable Tax Rate.

Add a Bundle Requirement to a Catalog Item

Search your catalog for an existing labor item for bundling. Make sure the assigned Item Type is the same type flagged as Labor and Bundle (as parent), as mentioned above. Scroll down until you see the Bundle Required field, and select this field. When you are finished, Save your changes.

Note: To add a new labor item, follow the standard Catalog Item Creation Process. After saving your changes for the new item, edit the catalog item, and the Bundle Required flag will appear.

Bundle Required Labor Items on Tickets

When you add a service/labor item to a ticket that requires a bundled item, you are notified when adding the item via the Smart Search feature as well as after the item is added to the ticket. The ticket can be saved without a bundled item, however, the option to move the ticket into Closed status is not allowed until a child item is bundled under the parent labor item.

Bundle Required Ticket Status

Once you’ve established a bundle requirement, you can also assign specific ticket statuses that restrict advancement until an item flagged as “Bundle Required” is added to the ticket. This ensures that tickets cannot advance until all required labor parts have been bundled.

From the Navigation Bar, go to Settings → Company Settings. On the Company Settings Overview page, scroll down to the Ticket section and click on General Settings.

Note: This feature is also available in Location Settings.

On the Ticket Settings page, scroll to the “Status Advancement Required Fields and Blockers” section. Turn ON the setting next to Prevent from Advancing to the Following Ticket Statuses and add the applicable ticket statuses in the Ticket Status field. When finished, Save your changes.
